Why Questions Break the Residual V2 Restriction (in Basque and Beyond)
Aritz Irurtzun
March 2021
 

Why questions behave specially in different languages (cf. i.a. Rizzi, 2001; Shlonsky & Soare, 2011). Here I bring into the cross-linguistic discussion evidence from Basque arguing that we should distinguish two types of why questions: (i) lower why questions where zergatik 'why' behaves like any other wh-word (wh-movement & T-to-C movement) and where the question queries about reasons or purposes (long or short construals), and (ii) higher why questions where zergatik outscopes the whole clause by first merge (involving no wh-movement & no T-to-C movement) and where the question queries the reasons why their factual complements take place (â‰ˆ English 'how come'; 'why is it that').
