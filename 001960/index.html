Quantifier float, focus, and scope in Thai
Peter Jenks
June 2013
 

In this paper I show that quantifier float in Thai shares several properties with QR, including locality restrictions and its effects on scope. I suggest that Q-float and QR share these properties because Thai Q-float is overt QR (cf. Jenks 2011). Q-float from both subjects and objects in Thai targets a position adjoined to vP, where quantifiers must take scope. I propose that Q-float in Thai involves movement of the entire DP, and that it is only the pronunciation of these elements which is discontinuous. I argue that Q-float is driven by information focus on the floated quantifier, following Simpson (2011). This is unsurprising, as other rightward movement phenomena such as heavy-NP shift and subject inversion are associated with focus on the rightward element.
