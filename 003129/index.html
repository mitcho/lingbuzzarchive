Syntactic ergativity as case discrimination
Amy Rose Deal
February 2017
 

I argue that ergative extraction restrictions arise from case discrimination –the requirement that the probe driving movement Agree only with a goal bearing a certain case. This proposal puts together the idea that A' movement is driven by heads which bear [EPP] and Agree in an OP(erator)-feature with Bobaljik’s (2008) proposal that Agree may be possible only for DPs with certain types of case. A ban on ergative A' extraction arises when the A' probe can agree only with unmarked DPs, not with DPs in dependent case.
