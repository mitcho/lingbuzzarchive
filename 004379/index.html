Licensing of PPI Indefinites: Movement or Pseudoscope?
Rajesh Bhatt, Vincent Homer
October 2018
 

Positive Polarity indefinites, such as some in English, are licensed in simplex negative sentences, as long as they take wide scope over negation. They can take wide scope either by movement or by some semantic mechanism, for example, they can take pseudoscope if they are interpreted as choice function variables. Therefore there is some uncertainty regarding the ways in which PPI indefinites get licensed: can pseudoscope suffice? In this article, we show, using novel data from Hindi-Urdu and English, that pseudoscope is not sufficient, and that it is the syntactic position of PPI indefinites at LF, rather than their actual scope, which is relevant for licensing. These facts support a unified view of PPI indefinites as generalized quantifiers, and disfavor analyses where they are, or can be, interpreted as choice function variables.
