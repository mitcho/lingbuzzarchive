Co-occurring plural marking and classifiers in Bishnupriya Manipuri
Shweta Akolkar
May 2023
 

It has often been claimed that numeral classifiers and plural markers are in complementary distribution crosslinguistically (T’sou 1976, Chierchia 1998, Borer 2005, i.a.). In a prominent account of this crosslinguistic pattern, Borer (2005) proposes that classifiers and plural markers are in morphosyntactic competition: both types of morphemes instantiate the same functional head and therefore cannot co-occur. The current paper evaluates the hypothesized complementarity between plural markers and classifiers in light of data from Bishnupriya Manipuri (Indo-Aryan; abbreviated to BPM), a language with a general-use plural marker that co-occurs with classifiers. I argue that the BPM plural marker is a Num head (in the sense of Ritter 1991), and classifiers occupy an adjacent Cl head in the nominal spine: both types of morphemes co-occur without either occupying a non-canonical position. The BPM data broadens the typology of number marking in classifier languages and indicates that a competition-based analysis cannot be maintained across languages.
