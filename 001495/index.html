Comparative Syntax
Richard Kayne
March 2012
 

The primary importance of comparative syntax lies in the fact that it provides us with new kinds of evidence bearing on questions concerning the general character of the language faculty. Figuring out what cross-linguistic generalizations hold and why exactly they hold will invariably help us to narrow down the set of hypotheses that we entertain about the language faculty. Comparative syntax in all its range can be seen as a window on the language faculty that is just beginning to bear fruit.
