Towards a Syntactic Understanding of Prosodically Reduced Pronouns
Kleanthes K. Grohmann
April 2000
 

Prosodically reduced pronouns come in two forms, weak and clitic, both differing from non-reduced strong pronouns. Both of these also occur in Westgermanic dialects and exhibit different properties which cannot always be unambiguously disentangled. The theoretical framework presented here allows us a straightforward disambiguation: reduced pronominal elements in the left periphery must be clitics, while those below cannot. The functional head F0, encoding point-of-view, serves as the licensing position for clitics which are here taken to be heads; weak pronouns, maximal phrases in the overt syntax, cliticize at LF. Movement to F0 is driven by the need to compensate for underspecified ?-feature information.
