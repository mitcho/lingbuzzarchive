RELABELING HEADS. A UNIFIED ACCOUNT FOR RELATIVIZATION STRUCTURES
Carlo Cecchetto, Caterina Donati
February 2010
 

We start from a specific approach to phrase structure theory (Cecchetto and Donati 2010) where a lexical item can transmit its label when it is merged with another category, both when it is externally merged and when it is internally merged (i.e. moved). This means that there is a type of movement, head movement, which has the property of relabeling the structure it merges with. Analyzing the raising of the relative head in different types of relativization structures as an instance of this relabeling head movement, we clarify the trigger of the movement, its relation to wh-movement, its case related properties, and its locality restrictions. We focus on full relative clauses, pseudorelatives and reduced relatives.
