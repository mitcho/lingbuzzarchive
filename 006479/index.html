On the Syntax of Mirativity: Evidence from Mandarin Chinese
Wei-Tien Dylan Tsai
February 2022
 

The aim of this paper is to investigate mirative construals in Mandarin Chinese, where the unexpectedness from the speaker's viewpoint is encoded by pure syntactic terms. Previous analyses focus mainly on languages with the evidential morphology (e.g., Gitksan, Tsafiki, etc), where indirect evidential markers can be used to express mirativity if the speaker directly witnessed the event. Mandarin, as a language without such morphology, expresses mirativity by employing either a sentence final particle a0 (i.e., a with the neutral tone), or sentential adverbials such as yuanlai 'turning out' and causal zenme 'how'. To provide a cartographic analysis, we distinguish MoodP from SAP, and split SAP further into two projections: the higher one (SAP1) indicates the relationship between the speaker and the addressee, while the lower one (SAP2) refers to the attitude of the speaker. We propose that declaratives and questions employ both, while miratives employ only SAP2, where the speaker's surprise is encoded. This leads to some very interesting results from the vantage point of the syntax-pragmatic interface.
