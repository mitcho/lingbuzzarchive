Wide-scope distributivity
Keny Chatain
September 2019
 

It is generally thought that distributive quantification obeys scope islands. Ruys (1992) notes that even when a plural indefinite can receive a wide-scope reading over an island, any distributive quantification associated to it cannot escape that island. In this paper, I investigate a particular class of exceptions to this generalization: distributive quantification of referential expressions (definites, proper names, etc.) can evade islands. Using dependent plurals as a diagnostic, I show that in these cases, the referential expressions are actually variables bound by a higher covert distributive operator. This operator acts on indices and thus needs not be local to the plurality it distributes over.
