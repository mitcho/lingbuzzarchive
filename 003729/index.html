Free relatives
Radek Simik
November 2017
 

This is a state-of-the-art article about the semantics of free relatives. Issues discussed include the basic denotation of free relatives (detailed comparison between the definite, universal, and indefinite positions), semantics of ever free relatives (expressing ignorance, indifference, or non-modal kinds of variation), and the issue of how free relatives get derived compositionally. The article is mostly a summary of what has been discovered in the past quarter of a century, but it also discusses novel crosslinguistic data, which suggest that the so called non-modal readings of ever free relatives seem to be most common crosslinguistically (despite the impression created by the literature on English free relatives).
