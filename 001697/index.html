A Fresh Look at Habitual Be in AAVE
Chris Collins
January 2006
 

In this paper, I will describe the use of agentive be in informal American English, basing my analysis primarily on my own intuitions (I-language) and sentences that I have found on the Internet. I will show that agentive be has largely the same syntactic behavior as habitual be in AAVE. Based on these similarities, I will conclude the paper by raising a number of questions about the origin of habitual be in AAVE. This is a pre-publication version. The published version appears in: Deumert, Ana and Stephanie Durrleman. 2006. Structure and Variation in Language Contact, pgs. 203-224. John Benjamins Publishing Company, Amsterdam/Philadelphia.
