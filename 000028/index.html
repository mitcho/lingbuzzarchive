Local agreement
Jan-Wouter Zwart
July 2004
 

The paper argues that agreement is an aymmetric relation between phrases (sisters), and that heads are only indirectly involved in agreement as terms of the dependent element on which the agreement relation is spelled out. It is argued that the c-command (AGREE) and spec-head relations are not sufficiently local, and that the variety of agreement phenomena requires that we identify sisterhood as the crucial agreement configuration.
