Cleaning up the lexicon
Michal Starke
November 2014
 

Terminological issues aside, there is near-universal agreement among linguists that the lexicon is a messy and ugly place. A place of disorder, exceptions and cacophony. This contribution is a short outline of why this consensus may prove to be wrong. More precisely, why it is vacuous on one reading, and probably wrong on the other. As an alternative, I will outline an approach with a clean, principled and restrictive lexicon, whose core is: The (morpho)syntactic lexicon contains nothing but well formed (morpho)syntactic expressions.
