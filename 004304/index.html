Using lexical semantics to predict the distributivity potential of Verb Phrases in a large dataset
Lelia Glass
November 2018
 

A predicate is understood distributively if it is inferred to be individually true of each member of a plural subject, as when "Alice and Bob smiled" conveys that they each did so. To address the open question of which predicates are understood distributively and/or nondistributively, this paper presents a dataset of quantitative ratings for the distributivity potential of 2338 Verb Phrases built from the verbs of Levin 1993. The dataset provides evidence for a series of hypotheses about how the nature of certain types of events shapes the inferences drawn from the predicates describing them.
