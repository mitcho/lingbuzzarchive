Anti-distributivity and predicate nominals
Nicholas Longenbaugh
July 2017
 

It is widely held that natural language makes available a covert alternative to the adverbial dis- tributive operator 'each', which I will refer to here as Dist. On an informal level, Dist combines with a predicate and a plural DP and asserts that each atomic element in the extension of the DP satisfies the predicate. Against this backdrop, Dotlacil (2011) observes the following puzzle: Dist is apparently unable to combine with plural subjects of predicate nominals (PNs): (1) *Several women are a doctor. I develop an account based on the observation that under the traditional view of plural number, applying Dist to the predicate derived by moving a plural DP results in a presupposition failure if the lower copy is plural. In order for movement to feed distributivity, it is therefore necessary for the lower copy not to contain a plural number feature. I argue that this can be accomplished via Takahashi and Hulseyâ€™s (2009) mechanism of wholesale late merge (WLM), which allows for reduced lower copies lacking a number feature. I then argue that the syntax of PNs independently blocks WLM of the subject, deriving anti-distributivity.
