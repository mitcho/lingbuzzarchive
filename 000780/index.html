Varieties of INFL: TENSE, LOCATION and PERSON
Elizabeth Ritter, Martina Wiltschko
September 2008
 

In this paper we argue that INFL is a universal category, which is associated with a core function, namely to anchor the reported event to the Utterance. While it us usually assumed that INFL is universally associated with the substantive content TENSE, we argue that the substantive content associated with INFL is in fact subject to parametric variation. While in English INFL is substantiated by TENSE, in Halkomelem (Salish) it is substantiated by LOCATION, and in Blackfoot (Algonquian) it is substantiated by PERSON.
