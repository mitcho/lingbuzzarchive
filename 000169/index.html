Nothing is a phonological fact
Curt Rice
May 2005
 

This is the handout from my talk at the Manchester Phonology Meeting 13, in May, 2005. The paper extends the approach presented in my "Optimal gaps in optimal paradigms" paper, an early version of which is on LingBuzz and the published version of which is on ROA. This handout discusses the possibility that the same phonological problem can turn up in two different word-formation processes in a language, and be dealt with differently in those two places, leading to a gap once and a repair the other time. This is shown to be a prediction of the "optimal gaps" approach, and the cases here provide an empirical basis for developing an evaluating that prediction. This will at some point become a paper, and news about that will be posted on http://www.hum.uit.no/a/rice .
