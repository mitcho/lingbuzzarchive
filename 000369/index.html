Deriving selectional properties of 'exclamative' predicates
Klaus Abels
November 2006
 

It is claimed that the fact that predicates like 'surprise' do not embed polar questions can be derived from the assumption that polar questions denote singleton sets of propositions together with independently needed presuppositions of 'surprise'. It is further claimed that a set of principled exceptions to the otherwise robust fact that predicates like 'wonder' do not embed intensified wh-questions suggests an account in terms of presuppositions. This work is part of a program to dissolve Grimshaw's (1979) clause-type features.
