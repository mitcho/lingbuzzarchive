On the pragmatic status of locally accommodated presuppositions
Omri Doron, Jad Wehbe
July 2024
 

In this paper, we investigate the nature of local accommodation by testing whether certain constraints on global accommodation generalize to local accommodation as well. We rely on a diagnostic first proposed by Orin Percus (1998) and Irene Heim (2015), which uses redundancy to probe for the pragmatic asymmetry between presupposed and asserted content in the global case. We provide novel data that show that even in the case of local accommodation, redundancy is sensitive to the difference between the two types of content. We argue that our data pose a problem for the A-operator theory of local accommodation, while being compatible with dynamic approaches (Heim, 1982) and a more recent domain restriction approach (von Fintel, 2004; Chatain and Schlenker, 2023).
