On licensing requirements of the Russian -nibud'-series
Natalia Fitzgibbons
February 2011
 

This paper discusses the distribution of the Russian nibud-series of non-specific indefinite pronouns. I show that nibud-items are not NPIs and argue that they are licensed by a variety of quantificational operators, many of which have been argued in the literature to be in the CP field. I discuss the licensing nibud by QNPs in detail and argue that the licensing QNPs are inherently presuppositional strong quantifiers. With respect to their syntactic position, I argue that nibud-items are adjoined to TP if they are objects or adjuncts and are possibly in Spec,AgrsP if they are subjects. Given the low scope requirement on the interpretation of -nibud'-items, I conclude that clausemate licensers of nibud in general are in the CP field. I also discuss the licensing of nibud-items in irrealis infinitives. I discuss data that show that Russian irrealis infinitives can be either CPs or TPs, and the ones where nibud-items are licensed are CPs. The licenser of nibud in these cases is similar to Stowells (1982) irrealis C.
