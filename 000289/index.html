Several, Few and Many (2nd draft)
Richard Kayne
June 2005
 

'Several', 'few', and 'many' have in common that they are modifiers of unpronounced NUMBER (and sometimes 'number'), closely related to modifiers like 'little' (in the case of 'several' and 'few') and 'large' (in the case of 'many'). 'Several' is more complex than 'many' and 'few' in necessarily entering into a larger comparative structure.
