On V2, gaps, and operators in comment and reporting parentheticals
Marlies Kluck, Mark de Vries
March 2014
 

Parenthetical comment and reporting clauses exhibit various interesting properties, which are shown to be related. Three different word order patterns are attested in Dutch and German: apparent V1, V2 and V-final; the internal argument of the parenthetical verb is usually missing; there is an optional so/zo element; the construction is island-sensitive. These patterns are explained by means of an operator that is AÂ´-moved inside the parenthetical, and which can optionally be lexicalized in the first position. The V-final pattern arises when a complementizer is present, which is the case in propositional as-parentheticals.
