Two disjunctions in Mandarin Chinese
Michael Yoshitaka Erlewine
July 2017
 

Revised as: 汨解沛ｼ "Interrogative and standard disjunction in Mandarin Chinese"

Mandarin Chinese has two disjunctors, haﾌ（shi and huoﾌzhe. Alternative questions use haﾌ（shi whereas logical, boolean disjunction is expressed with huoﾌzhe. Building on previous decompositional analyses of disjunction, I propose that haﾌ（shi spells out the junctor head J which projects its disjuncts as Roothian alternatives, whereas huoﾌzhe spells out a version of the J head that must be existential closed, forming a quantifier. This account contrasts from previous work on disjunction in Mandarin, which requires hﾃishi to move at LF or which requires the two disjunctors to differ in the size of disjuncts. Evidence comes from focus intervention effects and island (in)sensitivity.

I also consider environments where haﾌ（shi and huoﾌzhe are interchangeable, with disjunctive or conjunctive interpretation, which are also precisely where wh-phrases are used quantificationally. I offer a semantic characterization for these environments and argue against possible syntactic accounts. The distributions and interpretations of these disjunctors and wh-phrases in Mandarin Chinese form an argument for the two-dimensional Roothian Alternative Semantics framework over similar one-dimensional frameworks such as Hamblin semantics, from an empirical domain other than focus.
