Assessing the ability of Transformer-based Neural Models to represent structurally unbounded dependencies
Rui Chaves, Jillian Da Costa
December 2019
 

Filler-gap dependencies are among the most challenging syntactic constructions for computational models at large. Recently, Wilcox et al. (2018) and Wilcox et al. (2019b) provide some evidence suggesting that large-scale general-purpose LSTM RNNs have learned such long-distance filler-gap dependencies. In the present work we provide evidence that such models learn filler-gap dependencies only very imperfectly, despite being trained on massive amounts of data. Finally, we compare the LSTM RNN models with more modern stateof-the-art Transformer models, and find that these have poor-to-mixed degrees of success, despite their sheer size and low perplexity.
