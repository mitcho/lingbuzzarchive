The Nanosyntax of Czech Nominal Declensions

July 2022
 

This dissertation proposes an account of suffix allomorphy in the Czech nominal domain in the singular. Czech, among other Slavic languages, is notoriously known for its complex morphological system of nouns. Any attempt for an account has to answer at least two questions. First, what mechanism ensures the correct pairing of the root with its suffix. And second, what the relationship is between paradigm cells with the same form. This thesis combines the framework of Nanosyntax with Element Theory to shine a light on both inquiries and suggests that the daunting number of declensions is only apparent, and the much simpler, underlying system can be accounted for by using just standard Nanosyntactic tools.
