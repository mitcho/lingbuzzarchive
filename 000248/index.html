Newmeyer's rejoinder to Roberts & Holmberg on parameters
Frederick Newmeyer
February 2006
 

Roberts and Holmberg 2005 ('R&H') argue that Newmeyers arguments against [parametric theory] are based on misunderstandings either of theory or of data, are conceptually misconceived, illogical or simply false and represent a retreat to observational adequacy. In this rejoinder, I focus on the serious deficiencies in R&H. I argue in turn that R&H sets the bar for motivating parametric theory so low that it is hard to imagine what sort of data might refute it; that in more than one place R&H explicitly concedes the point argued for by Newmeyer; that R&H implies that by claiming (and reclaiming) that parametric theory is superior in principle to rule-based theory, nothing more needs to be said; that R&H adopts mutually incompatible assumptions; and that R&H ignores a key ingredient of Newmeyer's critique of parameters, namely the role of performance in accounting for cross-linguistic generalizations.
