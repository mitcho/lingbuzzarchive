The said and the unsaid: Learnability in the English Passives
Charles Yang
August 2018
 

I show that the English adjectival passive (*the read book vs. the unread book) is unproductive: the language learner needn't go beyond what is in the input data. The verbal passive, by contrast, is productive and the learner must generalize. The productivity calibration is done on the basis of the distributional data: If so, linguistic generalizations are be captured by a theory of learning and needn't be fitted into a theory of grammar.
