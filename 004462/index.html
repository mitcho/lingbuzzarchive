*What do you think that happens? A quantitative and cognitive modeling analysis of linguistic evidence across socioeconomic status for learning syntactic islands
Alandi Bates, Lisa Pearl
April 2019
 

While there are known differences in the quantity and quality of child-directed speech (CDS) at the lexical and foundational syntactic levels, far less is known about the development of complex syntactic knowledge and the input differences that may exist at this level across socio-economic status (SES). We investigate the differences in complex syntactic input in American English CDS between high-SES populations and low-SES populations, with respect to the distributions of wh-dependencies that reflect knowledge of syntactic island constraints. We then assess whether the low-SES wh-dependency distribution supports the acquisition of syntactic island constraints as well as the high-SES distribution has been shown to (Pearl and Sprouse, 2013). Via quantitative corpus analysis and cognitive modeling, we find that the nature of the input for learning about syntactic islands doesnâ€™t fundamentally differ across SES. We discuss implications for linguistic development across SES.
