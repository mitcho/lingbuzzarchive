Crossing and Stranding at Edges
Colin Davis
July 2018
 

This paper examines the distribution of stranding in phase edges under A-bar movement. I argue that the restrictions on this phenomenon provide evidence for the Cyclic Linearization theory of spellout, and a theory of movement as contingent on c-command-constrained Agree, which restricts movement in phrase edges. I show that these claims accurately predict a cross-linguistic word order generalization about stranding in intermediate positions, and provide insight into when a given position is viable for stranding or not. I go on to consider some extra-syntactic factors that further constrain stranding.
