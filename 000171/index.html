Against Person P
Daniel Harbour
May 2005
 

The predicate cleft (emphatic verb doubling) construction is developed as a diagnostic to T-to-C (V-to-C) movement in Classical Hebrew. The diagnostic is used to argue that split agreement (person-verb-number) does not arise from V landing between hypothetical Person and Number phrases; that is, PersonP and NumberP are not part of the Extended Verb Projection. The empirical focus is on the interaction between the predicate cleft and the syntax of questions, narrative forms (waw-conversum), imperatives and injunctives.
