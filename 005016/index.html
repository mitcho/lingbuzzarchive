Remarks on Shift Together
Amy Rose Deal
February 2020
 

This piece is an appendix to A theory of indexical shift (To appear, MIT Press). I discuss the formulation of the Shift Together constraint on indexical shift and why various purported counterexamples are not yet convincing.

Note that this is a prefinal version and page numbers are not those for the final book. The rest of the book can be found here: http://linguistics.berkeley.edu/~ardeal/papers/Deal-theory-of-indexical-shift.pdf

Those interested in Shift Together might also be interested in chapter 5 of the full book manuscript. That chapter discusses indexiphors, a type of phenomenon easily mistaken for indexical shift (and which can give the impression of Shift Together exceptions).
