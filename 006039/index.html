The nominal structure of clausal complements: An experimental study of wh-extraction in Bulgarian
Arthur Stepanov, Iliyana Krapova
June 2021
 

We explored the nominal structure of finite clausal complements of propositional attitude verbs in Bulgarian by examining potential complex NP island effects in wh-extraction out of these complements. We compared speakers' judgments and reading patterns concerning wh-extraction out of finite complements preceded by the overt correlative pronoun "tova" and out of complements in which no overt correlative is present, in an experimental study that combined sentence acceptability and self-paced reading tasks. We found that structure and dependency length affect acceptability of wh-dependencies with and without an overt correlative, but there was no additive effect. We also detected a preferential gradient bias for overt correlative across the tested verb classes. We conclude that both subtypes of clausal complements are essentially island-like configurations, which supports the nominal structure hypothesis for Bulgarian.
