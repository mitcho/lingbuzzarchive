Factivity-Alternating Attitude Verbs in Azeri
Tanya Bondarenko
August 2023
 

Factivity alternations received at least two kinds of explanations in the literature: there are approaches that attribute the two readings to two different LFs (e.g., Özyıldız 2017, 2018) and approaches that derive the presence/absence of a factive inference by appealing to general pragmatic mechanisms (Abrusán 2011; Beaver and Clark 2008; Jeong 2021; Simons et al. 2017). In this paper I investigate verbs displaying two different kinds of factivity alternations in Azeri (Turkic family) and argue for the former view of how factivity alternations emerge.
