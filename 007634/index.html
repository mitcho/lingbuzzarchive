Tamil pronominal alternations are phonology not allomorphy
Heather Newell
October 2023
 

In this article I present arguments for an analysis that offers an alternative to the account in Moskal (2015) and Moskal & Smith (2016) of Tamil pronominal alternations. These works have proposed that Tamil is a particular (although not the only) argument against a strict-adjacency (either structural or linear) account of suppletion, as suppletion is triggered non-locally, across the plural morpheme. If, however, the phonological evidence is taken into account, this example is not a case of suppletion at all, but rather falls out of the regular derivational and representational phonology of the language.
