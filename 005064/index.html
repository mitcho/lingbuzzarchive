The empty element in comparatives
Friederike Moltmann
March 1992
 

This paper is about the empty element in Comparative Deletion sentences such as 'John wrote more books than Bill read e.' This raises two questions. First, what is the syntactic status of the empty element? Second, how can the sentence be understood as 'John wrote more books than Bill read t-many books.' That is, how can the empty element e can be interpreted as 't-many books'? The answers that I will give to the two questions consist in an analysis of comparative deletion as antecedent-contained deletion based on a copying operation along the lines of May (1985) and Fiengo/May (1991) and on Haik's (1987) proposal according to which an operator may at S-structure bind a categorially inappropriate variable which is replaced by a semantically appropriate element only at LF.
