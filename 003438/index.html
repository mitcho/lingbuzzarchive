The Final-over-Final Condition and Processing
Michelle Sheehan
January 2017
 

This chapter considers potential accounts of FOFC based on processing in contrast with the syntactic or PF-interface accounts exported in other chapters of this book. Accounts based on Hawkins' (1994) Early Immediate Constituents and a generalised version of the Right Roof Constraint (Cecchetto 2013) are compared and contrasted and certain issues are raised with them. Finally an apparent parallel with the Onset Maximisation Principle in phonology is discussed. The conclusion is that purely processing-based accounts face certain problems but that (i) even if FOFC arises in the syntax or in its mapping to PF, the ultimate explanation may be processing-based (see also Abels and Neeleman 2012) and (ii) given the apparent similarity between FOFC and Onset Maximisation, no existing account is broad enough in its application.
