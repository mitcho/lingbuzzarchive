Yes, we have NoBanana?: Arguments against Richness of the Base
Charles Reiss
October 2024
 

Constraint based formalisms are useful in engineering domains, but it is not clear that they have any role in science. In this light it is worthwhile to re-evaluate the claimed results of constraint-based approaches to linguistics. The OT `principle' Richness of the Base (RotB) faces empirical, logical and conceptual challenges. RotB is based on the patently false claim that loanwords cannot introduce new sounds or sequences into `borrowing languages'. Smolensky 1996 shows that RotB entails an initial constraint ranking in which Markedness constraints outrank all Faithfullness constraints. Since this initial ranking has been shown to be untenable, RotB is untenable. Additionally, Tesar and Smolensky 2003 acknowledge that their constraint ranking learning algorithm works regardless of the initial ranking, which constitutes a tacit rejection of RotB as an important part of OT.
