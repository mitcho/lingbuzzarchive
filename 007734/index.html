Possessive prefixes across Algonquian: Evidence for two separate paradigms.
Ksenia Bogomolets
December 2023
 

In this paper, I argue that Algonquian languages provide ample synchronic evidence in favor of analyzing the alienable possessive prefixes (APP) and the inalienable possessive prefixes (IPP) as two separate paradigms. I argue that APPs and IPPs are underlyingly segmentally different: while the APP paradigm contains an underlying consonantâ€”/t, d/, the IPP paradigm does not. Crucially, I show that /t, d/ in APPs cannot be analyzed as epenthetic consonants to resolve vowel hiatus. Instead, I propose that the phonological behavior of these consonants represents a pattern of liaison. I show that such an analysis offers a better empirical coverage when compared to the earlier epenthesis accounts.
