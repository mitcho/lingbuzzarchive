Language, Chaos and Entropy
Diego Krivochen
March 2013
 

In this paper we will try to provide a formalization of some characteristics of language as a chaotic (physical) system aiming at justificative adequacy: what architectural properties license the occurrence of certain phenomena. We will claim that the derivational dynamics that can be found in language (and other systems of the mind) should be analyzed from the perspective of chaos theory, as a system which is hypersensitive to initial conditions: a small change in a certain state of the system may have drastic effects on the output, focusing on the consequences this approach would have for linguistic derivations and ontogenesis.
