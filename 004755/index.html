The semantics of comparisons in Mandarin Chinese
Linmin Zhang
February 2020
 

This paper analyzes the three fundamental uses of gradable adjectives in Mandarin Chinese: the positive use, the comparative use, and the measurement use. I argue that the notion of comparison underlies all these three uses, and thus the meaning of comparison does not need to be marked. This explains why, in Mandarin Chinese, there is no need to morphologically make a distinction between comparative and non-comparative forms. I analyze the semantics of gradable adjectives as a relation among (i) the measurement of an individual, (ii) a standard value for comparison, and (iii) the difference between the above two. The distinction among the three uses is attributed to their distinctive standard and difference. For the positive use, the standard is a typical average; for the comparative use, the standard is a contextually salient value; and for the measurement use, the standard is the absolute zero point of the relevant scale.
