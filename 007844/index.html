Allomorphy without Context Specification: A Case study of Czech Adjectival Stems
Pavel Caha, Karen De Clercq, Guido Vanden Wyngaerd
January 2024
 

This paper investigates stem-marker allomorphy in Czech adjectives. It argues that if accidental homophony is excluded, this allomorphy cannot be captured by the frequently used context-sensitive rules. The paper further demonstrates that the allomorphy can be accounted for within an approach based on portmanteau realisation of features, specifically the version of Nanosyntax proposed in Starke (2018), although alternative implementations are not excluded. Along the way, we explore a fine-grained decomposition of adjectival meaning and we also discuss the implications of these observations for the general issues surrounding the generative power of context-sensitive rules compared to other systems of dealing with allomorphy.
