The A-/A'-distinction as an epiphenomenon
Ken Safir
November 2015
 

It is argued that the A-/A’-distinction is an epiphenomenon emerging from independently necessary reformulation of Merge and the interpretive components. For the A-/A’-distinction to be truly derived, what distinguishes A-/A’-constructions must be deduced from independently motivated principles and the distinguishing factor should explain why the contrasts between A-/A’-constructions are precisely those contrasts and not others. A slight adjustment of the ‘Extension’ (Chomsky, 1995) meets these desiderata; by licensing penultimate merge, late merger of a complement-taking head to moved constituent becomes possible. This ‘insulating’ head, is always optional, but conspiring effects that predict where it can/cannot appear derive the A-/A’-distinctions.
