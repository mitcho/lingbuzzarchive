Sets, Heads and Spreading in LFG
Avery Andrews
April 2016
 

Andrews and Manning (1993, 1999) proposed to analyse a number of constructions, including complex predicates and modal adjectives, in a heavily modified version of LFG that could accomodate the fact that these constructions seem to involve more in the way of hierarchical structure than seemed to be supported by the LFG architecture. Here I show that the same results can be achieved by combining the 'hybrid objects' of the analysis of coordinate structures proposed by Dalrymple and Kaplan 2000 with a more extensive use of some of the capacities of LFG's glue semantics.
