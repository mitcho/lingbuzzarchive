Grounding with particles
Ahmad Jabbar, Veda Kanamarlapudi
August 2023
 

We focus on a sui generis grounding move in Hindi-Urdu dialogue, namely 'voh hi na'. 'Voh' is third person pronoun and can function as a propositional anaphor in dialogue. 'Hi' and 'na' are two discourse particles in Hindi-Urdu. A dataset consisting of minimal pairs of dialogues is presented to get a better sense of the move. Using dynamic models of discourse structure, we propose a semantics for 'voh hi na' in terms of its update effects. We propose a partly compositional account, where the string 'hi na' is taken to relate the antecedent proposition for the anaphor 'voh' to the discourse structure. Semantic accounts for 'hi' and 'na' in the literature can be tested for their ability to compositionally derive 'voh hi na''s meaning.
