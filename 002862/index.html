SEMANTIC GENDER DIVERSITY AND ITS ARCHITECTURE IN THE GRAMMAR OF ARABIC
Abdelkader Fassi Fehri
February 2016
 

Contrary to a widespread typology/theory of Gen(der), where Gen is essentially (a) a nominal class marking device, (b) semantically sex-based, and (c) syntactically reflected in gender agreement through sexed-animate controllers (Kibort & Corbett 2008; Dahl 2000; Kihm 2005, among others), I argue that Gen is (a) polysemous, instantiating a large and diverse number of meanings, (b) distributed over multiple layers in the DP, CP, or SAP architecture, and (c) it exhibits a variety of distinct controllers and properties of agreement.
