More Variation in Island Repair: Clausal vs. Non-Clausal Islands
Matthew Barros, Patrick D. Elliott, Gary Thoms
October 2013
 

An account of the systematic variation in the availability of so-called `island repair' effects in sluicing and fragment answers. We contribute a new empirical generalization: clausal islands show repair more readily than non-clausal islands. We argue that `island repair' is not in fact real, and that the clausal/non-clausal distinction is epiphenomenal, emerging on the basis of the availability of what we dub `island evasion strategies', i.e. strategies for avoiding island-violating movement in the ellipsis site. In the process, we show that the clausal/non-clausal distinction cross-cuts and thus falsifies proposed generalizations about the availability of repair.
