Clitic doubling in a determinerless language with second position clitics
Franc Lanko Marušič, Rok Zaucer
July 2009
 

It is typically the case that if a language exhibits clitic doubling, it has definite articles (see Bo¹koviæ 2008a,b,c) and it has verb-adjacent clitics. In this paper, we discuss the case of certain western dialects of Slovenian around Nova Gorica/Gorica/Gorizia, which we subsume under the cover term ‘Gorica Slovenian’. These dialects are interesting in that they appear to defy these generalizations. Whereas they have clitic doubling, their clitics are not verb-adjacent but second-position/Wackernagel clitics. Moreover, these dialects do not have a definite article. In what follows, we discuss how both of these facts are relevant for the claims that have been made in the literature on the basis of the above-mentioned generalizations.
