Marrying the prosentential theory of truth to formal semantics
Maksymilian DÄ…bkowski
May 2018
 

I propose a formal semantic account of the ordinary English word "true." The account is used to vindicate a modification of the prosentential theory of truth endorsed by Brandom (1994). "That is true," a paradigmatic prosentence, is analyzed as follows: "that" is treated as an anaphoric referent; "is true" is treated predicatively. With its predicative treatment of "is true," the account resembles a non-deflationist theory of truth (a "property" theory of truth). Despite the abandonment of the typical deflationist features, the expressive (as opposed to explanatory) nature of the account is argued for on the basis of the whole prosentence's compositional semantics. Formal aspects of the theory are used to show that Boghossian's accusations of conceptual instability do not apply.
