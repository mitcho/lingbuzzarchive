Ellipsis analysis for Mandarin Chinese Right Dislocation?
Yu-Chuan Lucy Chiang
March 2022
 

This study explores the formal syntax of right dislocation in Mandarin Chinese based on Taiwanese Mandarin. The present study argues that right dislocation in Mandarin Chinese may be different from some of these other languages. The ellipsis analysis might at first seem appealing for right dislocation in Mandarin Chinese, but some properties of the ellipsis approach to right dislocation in German and Dutch are not applicable to Mandarin Chinese. The discussion here suggests that there is variation in the syntactic operations that generate right dislocation in different languages, allowing the set of operations that generate right dislocation in MC to be different from the operations that generate right dislocations in languages such as German and Dutch.
