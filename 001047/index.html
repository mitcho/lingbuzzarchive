Negative DPs, A-movement, and Scope Diminishment
Sabine Iatridou, Ivy Sichel
May 2010
 

Reconstruction in A-chains has been disputed on various counts, including negative DPs (henceforth, NegDPs). Lasnik (1999) has claimed that NegDPs in derived subject position cannot be interpreted in the embedded clause and do not undergo A-chain reconstruction. We show that with a well-defined set of predicates, including deontic modals and raising predicates, scope diminishment of NegDP is observed. We argue, nevertheless, that it is not produced by A-chain reconstruction. We also show that A-reconstruction of the indefinite part is possible. We conclude that that the absence of NegDP reconstruction reduces to the absence of reconstruction of the negative ingredient, and suggest why this should be so. If we are correct, the analysis removes an obstacle to generalized reconstruction in both A and Ā-chains.
