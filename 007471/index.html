Subjunctive clauses as Weak NPIs in Russian
Tanya Bondarenko
August 2023
 

This paper investigates a case of polarity-sensitivity of certain subjunctive complements in Russian: with verbs like pomnit' 'remember', subjunctive complements behave like weak NPIs — they are possible only in Strawson Downward-Entailing (SDE) environments. I argue that polarity-sensitivity of these clauses in Russian arises because the subjunctive particle "by" comes with a focus feature from the lexicon, and activates subdomain alternatives of the predicate it combines with. These alternatives are later on acted upon by a focus operator (O-ALT), which requires that all the alternatives to the prejacent are entailed by it. This makes meanings of sentences with subjunctive complements under 'remember' logically trivial in cases when the sentence is not SDE with respect to the embedded proposition, giving rise to the polarity-sensitivity of subjunctive embedding. One consequence of this proposal is that clausal weak NPIs are treated on par with pronominal weak NPIs: the only difference between the two is that the latter are indefinite expressions ranging over individuals, whereas the former are indefinites ranging over situations.
