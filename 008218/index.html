Feature Bundling in the Left Periphery of Igbo Interrogatives
Jasper Jian
June 2024
 

Whether lexical items can bear more than a single feature of an extended projection remains an open question. If lexical items can bear only one feature, a derivational operation must occur whenever multiple features are hosted on a single head. In this paper, I argue to the contrary that distinct features can in fact be prespecified on the same lexical item. This is demonstrated on the basis of wh-in-situ questions in Igbo (Niger-Congo; Nigeria) which I show must involve presyntactically bundled A- and A'-features. This result also affirms that bundled features must be able to Agree with distinct goals while instantiating a single search. Furthermore, I provide a unified analysis of wh-in-situ and ex-situ questions which furthers our understanding of how the extended projection of the Igbo clause is structured.
