Everything's Indefinite
Jonny Butler
May 2005
 

I argue for a generalization of the existential-closure analysis for indefinites (Heim 1982) to cover all kinds of QPs: so that QPs uniformly introduce just a restricted variable into the representation, which is then closed off by intra-clausal quantificational heads analogous to the existential head proposed to sit on top of the VP by Diesing (1992); etc. (cf. Beghelli & Stowell 1997; Kratzer 2004; Sportiche 2005). This is implemented in a version of minimalist syntax that assumes the Inclusiveness Condition of Chomsky (2001), so co-identity is captured by a system of feature Agree rather than by co-indexation, deriving selectivity effects.
