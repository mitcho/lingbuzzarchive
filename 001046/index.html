Quirky Case and 'Co-generative' LFG+Glue
Avery Andrews
December 2010
 

In this paper I combine Andrews' (1982, 1990) 'two layer' analysis of Quirky Case in Icelandic with a version of LFG in which glue semantics serves as a 'co-generative' component, pairing meanings with f-structures that must match well enough with those produced by a standard LFG grammar that pairs f-structures with c-structures. This architectural change allows certain stipulative features of the original analysis to be eliminated, such as the use of the distinction between constraining vs. defining equations.
