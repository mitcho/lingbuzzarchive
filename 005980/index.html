Names as complex indices: On apparent Condition C violations in Thai
Khanin Chaiphet, Peter Jenks
October 2021
 

This paper provides a novel analysis of the ability of names and other R-expressions in Thai to violate Condition C. Two generalizations due to Hoonchamlong (1992) motivate the new analysis: 1) only a subclass of R-expressions in Thai can be bound and 2) those expressions require an identical binder. We derive these generalizations by proposing that the relevant class of noun phrases, including names, have the semantics of complex indices, analogous to first and second person pronouns (Minor 2011, Sudo 2012).
