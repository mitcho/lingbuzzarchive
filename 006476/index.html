No Gender in ‘Gender Agreement’: On Declension Classes and Gender in Serbo-Croatian
Boban Arsenijević
November 2021
 

The paper argues for a view of gender agreement without either grammatical or natural gender being represented as syntactic features. Rather than deriving declension classes in terms of realisation, I postulate them as the only relevant feature that is lexically specified on the noun. Agreement copies the declension class and triggers presuppositions. When these presuppositions clash with those already active in the discourse, default agreement is realised. The paper moreover provides a quantitative analysis of semantic correlates of declension classes and a novel division of the SC case endings across declension classes in light of syncretism.
