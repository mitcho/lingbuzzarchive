How to covertly move: Evidence from Passamaquoddy-Wolastoqey
Peter Grishin
December 2023
 

I introduce an understudied long-distance agreement construction in Passamaquoddy-Wolastoqey (Eastern Algonquian) and argue that it is derived by (potentially covert) raising to object. Then, I show that covert raising to object in Passamaquoddy-Wolastoqey can feed narrow-syntactic operations like agreement, A movement, as well as obviative marking. Thus, covert movement must be derived by lower copy pronunciation of a true narrow-syntactic movement dependency, rather than movement purely on the LF branch of a Y model of the grammar.
