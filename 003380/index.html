Exceptional cadential chords and tonal interpretation
Jonah Katz
March 2016
 

This paper presents an empirical generalization about exceptional cadential chords in Common Practice Period (CPP) tonal harmony, such as the augmented 6th and Neapolitan 6: they only occur as dependents of a dominant chord which is itself part of a full cadence. This pattern, which is difficult or impossible to account for in finite-state theories of musical harmony, is argued to be related to the 'tonic-marking' feature of a cadential dominant chord proposed by Katz & Pesetsky (2011). A sketch of an analysis is proposed involving the probe-goal theory of cadences and feature valuation.
