Clausal Complementation as a Compatibility Relation
Ken Safir
February 2021
 

The standard view of clausal complementation is asymmetric, namely, that predicates select the clause types they take as complements. I argue instead that clausal complementation is a compatibility relation that can involve saturation relations in both compositional partners. I argue that English inherent subjunctives (EIS)and Lubukusu actual clauses have an intrinsic semantics that limits the class of predicates they can combine with, but also can cause systematic shifts in the semantic interpretation of the clauses they combine with. In the case of EIS, variables in the left periphery of an EIS clause must be locally bound by a thematically appropriate binder in the argument structure of the predicate composed with it. Some predicates can accommodate binding by adding these thematic relations to their argument structure with a predictable shift in meaning. The argument is extended to Lubukusu actual clauses, which are complement clauses that impose an actuality entailment indexical to the utterer of the sentence.
