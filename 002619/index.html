Two ideas of creativity
Geoffrey Sampson
July 2015
 

Linguists use the terms "creative, creativity" in a way sharply different from usage in other domains of life. For linguistics, language behaviour is creative because the range of potential grammatical sentences is infinitely large. In ordinary usage, a creative activity is one whose products often fall outside any class that could have been predicted from past examples. This ambiguity has enabled generative grammar to be taken more seriously than it would be, if readers were conscious of the equivocation.
