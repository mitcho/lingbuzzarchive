On the Movement Theory of Control: Voices from Standard Indonesian
Yosuke Sato
May 2010
 

This paper presents morphosyntactic evidence against the movement theory of control espoused by Hornstein (1999) and defended in his subsquent work (Boeckx and Hornstein 2003, 2004, 2006). Standard Indonesian presents an important testing ground for this theory due to its syntatically governed distribution of the active voice morphology (Cole and Hermon 1998): the movement of an NP across a verb with the active voice prefix meN- results in the obligatory deletion of the prefix from the verb. In examples of obligatory control in this language, the prefix cannot be deleted but rather must be retained in the control verb. These examples thus provide important morphosyntactic evidence against the movement theory of control in Indonesian.
