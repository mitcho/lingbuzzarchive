The multiple grammaticalization of Romanian veni ‘come’. Focusing of the passive construction
Adina DRAGOMIRESCU, Alexandru Nicolae
January 2014
 

In Romanian, as in many other languages, the verb ‘come’ (Rom. 'veni') is polysemous,having multiple (related) meanings as well as various grammatical values. It is used as a passive auxiliary and as a copula in spoken contemporary Romanian, and as an inceptive verb in standard Romanian. The paper focuses on the history of the grammaticalization path from motion to passive, also discussing how the Romanian 'veni' passive is different from the cognate Italian 'venire' passive.
