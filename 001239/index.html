Particle-Stranding Ellipsis in Japanese, Phase Theory and the Privilege of the Root
Yosuke Sato
July 2011
 

In this squib, I present evidence that the Particle-Stranding Ellipsis, observed in casual dialogues in Japanese, is best analyzed as an instance of Rizzis (2005a) Privilege of the Root Phenomenon on a par with German topic drop. I propose that the stranding of the topic-marker wa arises when the topic NP undergoes movement into the specifier of the phase head Top in the left periphery, which triggers the Spell-Out of its complement domain to PF.
