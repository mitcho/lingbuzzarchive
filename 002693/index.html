TP is a phase in relative clauses
Amy Rose Deal
September 2015
 

I contend that TP is a phase in relative clauses, and this short handout explains why. The three core arguments are drawn from:
  1. Relative pronoun placement in Nez Perce
  2. Obviation of that-trace effects in English
  3. Highest subject restriction effects in Palestinian Arabic
In addition to supporting the phasehood of TP in relative clauses, the English and Palestinian case studies provide evidence that TP is not a phase in interrogatives or ordinary complement clauses. So, the phasehood of a projection must be in part a contextual matter.

Further discussion of these arguments, especially 1 and 2, is in my paper "Cyclicity and connectivity in Nez Perce relative clauses" (To appear, LI), which you can download here.
