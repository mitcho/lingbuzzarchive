Pre-DP 'only' is a propositional operator at LF: a new argument from ellipsis
Itai Bassi, Aron Hirsch, Tue Trinh
January 2023
 

English "only" can appear in various syntactic positions, including at the clausal spine ("only [drink WINE]") or attached to a noun phrase/DP ("drink [only WINE]"). Different theorists diverge on whether the analysis of the latter, pre-DP use of 'only' should be reduced to the analysis of the former, pre-VP use of it at the level of Logical Form. We provide a new argument (following Benbaji 2022) in support of the view that the meaning associated with 'only' always come from a propositional (clausal) operator at LF, despite surface appearances in the case of the pre-DP use. The argument is based on the scope possibilities of pre-DP 'only' relative to modals and their interaction with ellipsis.
