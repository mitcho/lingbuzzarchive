Character Study: A de se semantics for indexicals
Craige Roberts
April 2020
 

For indexicals, both the classical directly referential semantics of Kaplan (1977) and more recent anaphoric accounts offer a picture of indexicality which is empirically and conceptually inadequate. They fail to capture this fact: Indexicals are essentially perspectival, as reflected in the fact that 1st and 2nd person indexicals are always de se. After giving evidence that this is the case, I develop an account of indexicality based solely on independently motivated elements required for an adequate account of the de se and of doxastic anchoring in deictic motion verbs and epistemic modals. Then I use these tools to develop a detailed formal semantics for English 'I' and 'we', and to sketch how shifted indexicals like the Amharic first person singular pronoun and English 'now' work naturally in the present framework.
