Evidence for Tense Heads in Mandarin Chinese (Beijing Dialect)
Xuan Di
February 2007
 

The existence of sequence of tense phenomenon in Beijing dialect calls for a syntactic Tense node. Temporal words such as jing-tian to-day are defined as Deictic, as they cannot modify a shifted time in past embedding past tense. When san tian qian three days ago/before, is used to modify the shifted time, they are non-deictic words with non-deictic meaning. The internal structures of the two types of temporal phrases are examined. Derivations on how the CP and its parallel DP are licensed are given. T-to-C movement is argued to exist only in non-deictic words.
