Parsing Out in English and Vietnamese
Nigel Duffield
October 2018
 

Here I outline an alternative approach to two types of minimal contrast in sentence acceptability that have previously been described (and explained) in terms of abstract formal grammatical mechanisms, namely, that-trace effects in English, and wh-island effects in Vietnamese. This alternative treatment—parsing out—is argued to be preferable to standard analyses, inasmuch as it makes use of independently needed processing principles to yield at least equivalent empirical coverage.
