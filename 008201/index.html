Flavors of -ga: A possession class suffix in O’dam
Michael Everdell
August 2017
 

This paper describes attributive possession in the O'dam language of Durango, Mexico based on the author's original fieldwork. In particular this paper focuses on the properties of the -ga suffix which other related languages (Tepiman < Uto-Aztecan) use to mark alienable possession. I show that O'dam has lost an active alienablity distinction and instead only shows a relic of the older system in its possession classes, characterized by the distribution of the -ga suffix. Finally, I provide a diachronic analysis of the -ga suffix and possessive pronouns in the Tepiman subgroup of Uto-Aztecan.
