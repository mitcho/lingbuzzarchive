In Defense of Vacuous Projections in Bare Phrase Structure
Maximiliano Guimaraes
June 2000
 

According to the current minimalist view, Bare Phrase Structure Theory (Chomsky 1994, 1995) implies the impossibility of vacuous (non-branching) projections. The problem with that is that the existence of structures with two heads in symmetric c-command relation at the very bottom of the phrase marker is inevitable, making impossible the linearization of the terminals according to the LCA (Kayne 1994). Assuming the Bare Phrase Structure Theory in all its aspects, I argue in this paper that vacuous projections are totally compatible with the minimalist desiderata, being available for the system as a legitimate (last resort) strategy to avoid symmetric c-command. Consequently, the linearization problem is just illusory.
