Potential possibility: The Tlingit potential meets formal semantics
James Crippen
April 2012
 

I argue that the potential mode of Tlingit verbs is a method of expressing pure possibility modality. I document the morphological and syntactic essentials of the potential mode, showing that it is morphologically limited to the verb and it is syntactically not isomorphic with the subjunctives of European languages. I offer a preliminary semantic definition of the potential operator POT, demonstrate that contra Leer (1991) negation can scope over or under POT, and show from textual and elicited data that it does not encode any particular conversational background.
