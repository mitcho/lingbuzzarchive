The Syntax and Syncretisms of the Person Case Constraint
David Adger, Daniel Harbour
June 2006
 

The Person Case Constraint is frequently concomitant with Case Syncretism. We provide a syntax-driven account of both phenomena that relies on the dual role that phi features play in selecting and in Case licensing argument DPs. The account differs from other syntactic approaches to the PCC in the role it affords the applicative head in the Case system and in the attention it pays to the syntactic structures that feed morphology and therefore induce syncretism. (NB an earlier version of this paper appeared in MITWPL. This revised version improves on the theoretical approach in a number of respects in section 5)
