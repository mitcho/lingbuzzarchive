Ellipsis in Additive Responses
David Adger
July 2017
 

In examples like (1) and its negative counterparts, a simple analysis involving Subject Auxiliary Inversion as in Wood 2008 is inadequate, since it is possible to have multiple auxiliaries preceding the subject as in (2) (Culicover and Winkler 2008). (1) Lilly caught a frog. So did Dodger. (2) His hair was light, and so would have been his complexion. The equivalents in Scottish Gaelic involve conjunctions and I develop an analysis of these arguing that they involve ATB extraction of the verb. I then show how that analysis can be extended to the English facts and places the auxiliary in a separate constituent to the subject. This explains the possibility of multiple auxiliaries preceding the subject and provides a unified analysis for the two languages (and, I suggest, for a similar case in Japanese).
