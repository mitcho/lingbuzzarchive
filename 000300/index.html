Icelandic Case Fluctuation and Movement into Theta-Positions
Dorian Roehrs
February 2004
 

This paper studies case fluctuation on the matrix subject of the reflexive middle verbs 'kveðast' and 'segjast' in Old and Modern Icelandic. While some speakers accept the nominative case on the matrix subject, other do the inherent case assigned in the embedded infinitival. Showing that the matrix predicate exhibits control properties only, I argue that inherently case-marked subjects have moved into a theta position.
