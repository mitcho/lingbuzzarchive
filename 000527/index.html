Uncharted territory? Towards a non-cartographic account of Germanic syntax
Jan-Wouter Zwart
October 2007
 

This article discusses the consequences of a strict derivational approach혰where syntactic relations are construed dynamically as the derivation proceeds혰to the analysis of key areas of Germanic syntax. It discusses the nature of syntactic positions from a non-cartographic point of view. Evidence supporting a non-cartographic approach is found in word order transitivity failures in various domains (the left periphery, the order of adverbs, the adjective-noun construction). The implications of a non-cartographic approach are discussed in four key areas of Germanic syntax (the fine structure of the left periphery, topicalization/focalization, subject placement and object placement).
