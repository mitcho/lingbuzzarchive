Alternating conj/disjunctions: the case of Japanese -toka and -tari
RyanWalter Smith, Ryoichiro Kobayashi
February 2018
 

This paper investigates the interpretation of Japanese -toka and -tari, two non-exhaustive particles that receive conjunctive interpretations in upward-entailing environments, but disjunctive interpretations in downward-entailing and question contexts. We analyze -toka and -tari as items that introduce unstructured sets of alternatives in a single-tier alternative semantics (Hamblin 1973; Kratzer and Shimoyama 2002), and derive their conjunctive and disjunctive readings via an interaction between these sets and the semantics of the environment containing them.
