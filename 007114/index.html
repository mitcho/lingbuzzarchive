18-month-olds understand the links between declaratives and assertions, and interrogatives and questions
Daniel Goodhue, Valentine Hacquard, Jeffrey Lidz
February 2023
 

Matrix declarative clauses are typically used to assert information, while matrix interrogative clauses are typically used to ask for information. We developed a novel preferential looking study to determine when children learn this, with a focus on the second year of life. Participants watch a video of two puppets. One lacks relevant information while the other has it. At test, the puppets face one another, and participants hear either a series of declarative-assertions or interrogative-questions. The puppets mouths don't move, so participants need to determine who is speaking based on context. We infer who participants believe to be speaking from which puppet they look at. If children understand the links between clause types and speech acts, they should look at the uninformed puppet more during interrogatives, and the informed puppet more during declaratives. Our results demonstrate an effect in the expected direction for 18-month-olds.
