Regularity and lexical diffusion in phonological change
Samuel Andersson, Ollie Sayeed, Bert Vaux
March 2022
 

There has been a tension in the historical linguistics literature between two different accounts of phonological change: regular Neogrammarian sound change and lexical diffusion. This chapter provides a summary and analysis of this debate, but also problematizes previous theoretical interpretations. First, we introduce the historical background to the debate about the regularity of sound change, and then discuss the evidence for lexical diffusion and for Neogrammarian change. We answer some questions about diffusion: when it occurs, how it spreads, and whether it eventually leads to regularity. We then move on to a more theoretical perspective of sound change, discussing the relationship of Neogrammarian change and diffusion to synchronic phonological theories, particularly stratal models and exemplar theory.
