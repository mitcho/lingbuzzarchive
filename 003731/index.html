Plural Reference and Reference to a Plurality. Linguistic Facts and Semantic Analyses (OUP 2016)
Friederike Moltmann
April 2024
 

There are two approaches to the semantics of plurals: reference to a plurality 'as one' (a sum or set of entities) and plural reference, reference to several things at once. Whereas the first view is standard among linguists, the second view has been pursued mainly by philosophical logicians (Yi, McKay, Oliver and Smiley). The paper argues in favor of plural reference on the basis on linguistic data. It also gives a novel account of higher-order plurality (superplurals), and it argues for a sharp distinction between the notion of a single thing (such as a sum or set) and an integrated whole, which can be a maximally connected plurality 'as many'. The distinction plays a role in semantic selection of plural predicates: plural predicates divide into whether they select the former or the latter.
