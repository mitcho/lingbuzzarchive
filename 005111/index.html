A Short Note on English long and the Why of Negative Polarity
Richard Kayne
March 2020
 

There is an answer to the question why bare 'long' and 'a long time' differ in the way they do, with 'a long time' showing no need for negation at all. The reason is that 'a long time' contains no silent TIME in need of licensing. The apparent negative polarity status of bare 'long' is, indirectly, just a side effect of its containing TIME.
