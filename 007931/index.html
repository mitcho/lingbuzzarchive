An experimental investigation of the clausemate condition in German multiple sluicing
Álvaro Cortés Rodríguez, James Griffiths
January 2023
 

This study investigates the clausemate condition (CC) in multiple sluicing (MS). The CC is the observation that in MS configurations with biclausal antecedents are judged as highly degraded / unacceptable when at least two of the sluiced wh-phrases’ correlates in the antecedent utterance are separated by a finite clause boundary (Takahashi 1994, Nishigauchi 1998, Merchant 2001, Lasnik 2014, Abels & Dayal 2022). Nevertheless, acceptable apparent obviations of the CC seem to be attested (e.g., Nishigauchi 1998, Lasnik 2014, Grano & Lasnik 2018, Barros & Frank 2022). The most prominent of those exceptional cases is a biclausal configuration with a bound pronominal subject in the embedded clause. The current study aims to provide a more robust empirical foundation for recent claims made about the CC, both from Barros & Frank, Abels & Dayal, and Grano & Lasnik 2018. In particular, it reports a formal experimental acceptability study on configurations in German where correlate1 (dative complement of the matrix clause) and correalte2 (prepositional complement of the embedded clause) are separated by a clause boundary. Additionally, the factor of SHIFTINESS is included to test the availability of correlate one to bind into a subject or object pronoun in the embedded clause. These findings diverge from Barros & Frank's discourse-centered solution and suggest true CC obviations may stem from processing rather than discourse factors.
