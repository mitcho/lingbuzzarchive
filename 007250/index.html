Intervention effects and phase boundaries in Czech clitic climbing
Guy Tabachnick
September 2022
 

This paper examines restrictions on the ability of Czech second-position clitics to climb out of embedded clauses. Clitics cannot climb out of CP phases, but can climb out of infinitives without CP phase boundaries, even those with syntactic subject PRO. Clitics cluster together in a set order, and while arguments of a single verb can freely rearrange themselves to match the required order, an argument of an embedded infinitive generally cannot climb over the controller in the matrix clause in object control constructions. I propose that clitic movement is due to a DP probe that comes equipped with a hierarchy of case features, and that clitics reached by the probe out of order with respect to the hierarchy are trapped and cannot cliticize. Arguments may freely scramble within a single TP, allowing arbitrary reordering, but embedded arguments cannot scramble over matrix arguments, leading to the restrictions in object control sentences.
