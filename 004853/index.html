"U" in Taiwanese Southern Min existential and possessive predicate constructions
Julia Julia Skrobol
April 2019
 

The focus of this pap er is u in Taiwanese Southern Min existential and possessive predicate constructions. A central question regarding u(s) in those structures is whether they should be treated as two lexically distinct copular verbs, or rather two realizations of the same morphological element. Tied to this question is the syntactic position and semantic content of u in those constructions. Drawing on previous approaches to possessive predication, particularly the notion of be- and have- languages, I argue that u in existential and u in possessive predicate should be analysed as the same copular verb. By adopting Freeze’s (1992) D- structure of the unified ‘Locative Paradigm’ I show that u in existential and u in possessive predicate derive from the same underlying structure and, consequently, are fundamentally the same copular verb. I also show that the existential and possessive predicate meaning arises from the configuration of those structures and not u itself.
