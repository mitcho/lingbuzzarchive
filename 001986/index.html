VP anaphora and verb-second order in Danish
Line Hove Mikkelsen
December 2013
 

This paper argues that Danish verb-second clauses come in two structural varieties. One variety is a CP structure in which Spec-CP is filled with an element that carries some C-related function, expressing either illocutionary force or information structure. The other variety is a TP structure in which the subject occupies Spec-TP and there is no CP projection. The choice between the two structures is governed partly by information structure, in particular whether the subject is information structurally distinguished, partly by the presence of an overt VP-anaphor, which forces a CP projection above TP.
