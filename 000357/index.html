The Enhancement/Repression of Phasehood
Shin'ya Uchishiba
October 2006
 

The aim of this paper is to contribute to the phase theory (Chomsky 2000, et preq) by modifying it so that it can suit to more empirical facts. My proposal is that phases must conform to some syntactic conditions in order to behave as such. Two conditions are put forth. One is that the specifiers of phases must be occupied by some element, and the other that their complements must be headed by semantically independent terms. When phases satisfy these conditions, they are enhanced; otherwise, they are repressed. In terms of this phase system, I examine extraction from DP and that from CP. The system is shown to accont for these two sorts of extraction in a unified way.
