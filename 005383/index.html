Proposing to ignore discourse updates in Colloquial Singaporean English (CSE)
Robert Henderson, Jian Gang Ngui
August 2020
 

In this paper, we use Farkas and Bruce’s (2010) Table model to provide a unified formal semantic account for the apparently disparate discourse effects of the Colloquial Singaporean English discourse particle ló (Gupta 1992; Wee 2002; 2010). We consider three core cases involving ló: (i) when a request is made; (ii) when discourse participants are committed to contradictory propositions; (iii) when a question is asked. We propose that ló always lays out two conversational futures. In the first, the prejacent has its standard effect. In the second, the conversational participants agree to ignore their last moves via a discourse update we term Wipe. The listener must then decide which path to take, just like when a listener selects a proposition (an answer) from a set of propositions (a question). We show that our proposal can account for how a ló-marked utterance brings about agree-to-disagree effects, which has so far gone unnoticed in the literature, while also successfully capturing previous descriptions of ló, which includes expressing resignation on the part of the speaker or marking an utterance as being obvious.
