Syntax of Classifier Incorporation in Indian Sign Language
Samar Sinha
March 2005
 

In Indian Sign Language (ISL), signer has a choice to incorporate classifier handshape into verb or to sign verb in its citation form, which is assumed to be governed by the discourse-pragmatic factor. ISL classifiers reflect visual image of the referent at the moment of transfer indicated by verb, and quantifier and numerical lack co-occurrence. The choice of classifier handshape that incorporates into verbs of motion and location depends upon the quantification making a bipartite division between MANY vs. rest. This shows that the incorporation of the classifier handshape into verb is the incorporation of the highest specifier of the phrase that cross-classifies as well as quantifies a noun, which is syntactically governed by the verb.
