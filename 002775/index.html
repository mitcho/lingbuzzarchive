Neglect (or doing away with Late merger and Countercyclicity)
Dominique Sportiche
November 2015
 

The key general proposal is that the principle of Full Interpretation applies to syntactic objects rather than their occurrences (for example to a chain – a set of movement copies - rather individual copies). This conception derives the existence of interpretive Neglect, that is the possibility of interpretively ignoring up to all occurrences of an interpretable syntactic object but one. This subsumes the effects of assuming Late Merger. In addition, Late Mergers of various kinds are also discussed and shown not to be independently motivated.
