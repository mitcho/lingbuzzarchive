Negation, polarity and deontic modals
Sabine Iatridou, Hedde Zeijlstra
September 2012
 

Whereas existential deontic modals (may, can) always scope under negation, universal deontic modals may vary w.r.t. whether they scope over or under negation. Modals like "must" and "should" take wide scope w.r.t. negation, modals like "have to" and "need to" take narrow scope. Similar patterns have been attested in other languages. In this article we argue that the scopal properties of modals w.r.t. negation can be understood if (i) modals that outscope negation are Positive Polarity Items (PPIs); (ii) all modals originate in a position lower than IÂ°; and (iii) modals undergo reconstruction unless reconstruction leads to a PPI licensing violation. Finally, in this article the question is addressed as to why certain modals are PPIs and others not.
