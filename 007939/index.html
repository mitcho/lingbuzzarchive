O gradience, where art thou? Examining backness harmony in Uyghur
Adam McCollum, Karthik Durvasula, Xiayimaierdan Abudushalamu
February 2024
 

Discreteness of representations is a core commitment of classical generative phonology. However, one of biggest arguments levied against discreteness has come from the phenomenon of incomplete neutralization – where the output of some phonological process on a feature [F] is phonetically distinct from underived [±F]. A sizeable body of work has argued that incomplete neutralization crucially undermines the sufficiency of discrete representations, instead contending for the inclusion of gradient representations. In this paper we investigate backness harmony in Uyghur, evaluating recent claims of incomplete neutralization. Results from two production studies replicate findings from previous work on the language, but we show that, with proper experimental controls, the pattern is derivable without recourse to gradient phonological representations. We then discuss the conceptual and theoretical challenges for gradient representations along with criteria for true phonologically-relevant gradience.
