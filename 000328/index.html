Eager for Distinctness
Raj Singh
August 2006
 

I provide evidence that the interpretation of disjunctive sentences is sensitive to the linear order of the disjuncts. I argue that the asymmetry is rooted in informational redundancies that are present in one order that are not present in the other. I propose a constraint that ensures informational distinctness between earlier and later disjuncts. The constraint is checked at a surprising point in the left-right interpretation of the sentence, hence making the dynamics of the interpretive process of crucial importance in accounting for the observed asymmetry.
