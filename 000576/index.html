The Denotation and Morphosyntax of Bare Nouns in Javanese: Against the Nominal Mapping Parameter
Yosuke Sato
January 2008
 

In this paper, I argue against Chierchia¡¯s (1998a, b) Nominal Mapping Parameter (NMP) from Javanese and develop an alternative, purely syntactic account of the effects that the NMP was designed to capture. I first show that Javanese poses an empirical challenge to the NMP because this language does not fit into any one of the three language types that should exhaustively classify all natural languages under Chierchia¡¯s typology. I propose a new parametric syntactic account, whereby languages differ with respect to how high a language allows its bare nominal to grow, drawing on the related ideas independently argued for in recent work as in Grimshaw (1991), Massam (2001), and Guilfoyle and Noonan (1992). I show that this parametric analysis of the height of the nominal functional structure derives a variety of morphosyntactic properties of bare nominals across languages, including those noted by Chierchia, in tandem with the independently motivated language-particular values for Number: {singular, plural} vs. {neutral. plural}.
