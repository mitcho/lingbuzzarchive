Syntax-prosody Mapping of Right-Dislocations in Cantonese and Mandarin
Ka-Fai Yip
March 2020
 

[published in Phonological Externalization (Vol. 5): https://sapporo-u.repo.nii.ac.jp/?action=repository_uri&item_id=7728&file_id=22&file_no=1]

This paper argues for the one-to-one syntax-prosody mapping of CP and C0 with novel evidence from right-dislocations (RDs) in Cantonese and Mandarin. From acoustic experimental results, RDs have one intonational phrase (ι-phrase), which is compatible with the well-received monoclausal analysis for Chinese RDs: one CP is mapped to one ι-phrase. Afterthoughts with "copied" intonations in Cantonese are proved to be an instance of mapping two CPs to two ι-phrases. Besides, variations in having boundary tones, "copied" intonations and the SP requirement of RDs are predicted to be correlated under the current proposal.
