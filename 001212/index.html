The modality of (im)perfectivity
Carlo Linares
November 2010
 

Sentences in the perfective aspect are anchored in the world of evaluation, whereas sentences in the imperfective seem to be evaluated, at least primarily, with respect to a background set of possible developments, or inertia worlds. In this squib, I show that the apparent difference between these viewpoint aspects in terms of modality does not have to be stipulated in the lexical entry of the relevant items, and that the anchoring of the perfective in the world of evaluation follows naturally under the assumption that viewpoint aspect has a uniform modal component involving quantification over inertia worlds.
