Two languages, two minds? Horrifying Schrödinger
Jose-Luis Mendivil-Giro
December 2016
 

It seems that the ancient intuition that language lies behind consciousness and the nature of the human mind makes sense. But, contrary to the suggestion by Athanasopoulos et al. (2015), the ingredients that form the fabric of our human mind are not the superficial, external aspects of language that differentiate German from English (i.e. those aspects subject to historical change and, therefore, variation), but what is common to all languages, including, of course, the various languages in the (single) mind of a bilingual.
