The interaction of Th/Ex and Locative Inversion
Milan Rezac
November 2004
 

Th/Ex is the operation posited to explain the pre-participial position of the object in (ii) vs. (i): (i) _Kate has <*three fish> caught _, _There were caught <*three fish>_. Th/Ex has been variously formulated with importance to recent theorizing: phase-edge movement, phonological operation, participle movement, etc. In this squib I bring to bear on the issue evidence from the interaction of Locative Inversion with Th/Ex: LI blocks Th/Ex, (iii) _In the lake were <*three fish> caught _. (iii) falls into place if Th/Ex is A-movement to [Spec, PRT] (Lasnik 1995, Holmberg 2002), which LI pre-empts by filling [Spec, PRT] by the inverting locative. On the other hand, (iii) presents a serious problem for partciple-raising (Caponigro and Sch√ºze 2003) and phonological operation (Chomsky) accounts of Th/Ex, and a near paradox for theories which assimilate expletives to inverting locatives or posit covert expletives in Locative Inversion (Hoekstra and Mulder 1990).
