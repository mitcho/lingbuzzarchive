Ineffable narratives. Another case of overgeneration by e-GIVENess

November 2014
 

In this paper, I present a new case of overgeneration to the semantic-pragmatic view on identity in ellipsis. Concretely, I show that a radical version of the semantic approach to the identity condition on ellipsis, in particular, one with the notion of pragmatic entailment at its heart, wrongly predicts as grammatical cases of TP-ellipsis in Spanish where a (formal) present tense feature on T in the antecedent undoubtedly entails (in the above sense) a (formal) past tense feature in the elliptical constituent and viceversa. However, this is not attested: present tense cannot serve as a suitable antecedent for formal past tense in TP-ellipsis contexts, regardless of pragmatic entailment.
