Failed clitic doubling of plural indirect objects in Greek
Maria Kouneli, Yuriy Kushnir
August 2022
 

Greek allows Clitic Doubling (CD) of both direct objects (DOs) and indirect objects (IOs). While the phenomenon is well-studied (see Anagnostopoulou 2017 for an overview), there is a restriction in the CD of IOs which has been largely ignored in the literature (but see Anagnostopoulou 2017, fn.18): CD of plural IOs is significantly degraded, with judgments ranging from strongly to mildly ungrammatical depending on the speaker. In this paper, we show that the ungrammaticality of CD of 3rd-person plural IOs is due to a morphological mismatch between the clitic 'tus' and the determiner 'ton' in the plural, where - unlike in the singular - the form of the clitic and the article are not identical. We provide an analysis, and we argue that the data strongly support movement-based theories of CD, in particular those in which the clitic and the CDâ€™ed object form a movement chain (e.g. Anagnostopoulou 2003, Harizanov 2014, Nevins 2011, Preminger 2019).
