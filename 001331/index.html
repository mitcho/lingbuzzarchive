Genitive of Dependent Tense in Japanese and Its Correlation with Genitive of Negation in Slavic
Shigeru Miyagawa
September 2011
 

In Slavic, the genitive case may occur on the internal argument of a predicate — subject of unaccusatives and passives, and on direct objects — when there is negation. This is the so-called genitive of negation. In this paper I argue for genitive case in Japanese that has essentially the same distribution as the genitive of negation. The one difference is that what licenses the genitive in Japanese is not negation, but a form of tense, which I label as dependent tense, which occurs in certain embedded environments. I show that this genitive is fundamentally different from the genitive that is licensed by the D head (Miyagawa 2011) or possibly nominalized C (Hiraiwa 2005). Among the differences, the genitive of dependent tense occurs in a full CP while the D-licensed genitive occurs in “reduced” TP.
