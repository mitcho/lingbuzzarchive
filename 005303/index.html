A semantic analysis of embedded tense in finite control in Japanese
Yusuke Kubota, Koyo Akuzawa
July 2020
 

Akuzawa and Kubota (2019) propose a semantic analysis of finite control in Japanese. But their analysis does not consider the temporal relations between the control predicate and the embedded predicate in detail, and there are some important empirical data regarding the status of the embedded tense and the temporal interpretations of the embedded clause discussed in the literature which are left unaddressed in their work. The present paper addresses this important remaining issue in the semantic analysis of finite control in Japanese, focusing on koto-taking control verbs. Specifically, we show that once we make explicit the lexical semantic properties of control verbs pertaining to the temporal relations between the matrix and embedded events, the observations which have (sometimes) been taken to support the defectiveness of embedded tense fall out straightforwardly as a consequence of the inherent semantic properties of the control verbs.
