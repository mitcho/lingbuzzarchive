Determiner Spreading as DP-Predication
Phoevos Panagiotidis
July 2007
 

Determiner Spreading (DS) is a type of adjectival modification that involves more than one definite article and so far has only been extensively described in Greek. This paper discusses the properties of DS by comparing it to similar constructions in Semitic, Albanian and Mainland Scandinavian. We argue that DS is a configuration involving DP predication and serving both as an argument and as a predication structure. In the last part of the paper we provide data on the acquisition of DS by children acquiring Greek which support this analysis over some of its well-known alternatives in the literature.
