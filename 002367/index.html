Where not to put why, and why not
Nigel Duffield
January 2015
 

This article—an excerpt from a larger manuscript (Duffield, in prep.)—examines four classes of paradigmatic exception to the putatively general rules of English wh-movement. I argue that these distributions, particularly the fourth, *‘She wondered why to go there’ challenge the standard generativist idea that syntactic gaps are the invariable result of innate negative constraints. Rather, they suggest that generalizations about ‘wh-movement’ are better handled in terms of a theory of questions that is highly sensitive to properties of the input, especially type frequency. The data also provide additional empirical support for the idea that grammaticality judgments have an external, rather than internal, source (e.g. Brooks & Tomasello 1999, Theakston 2004, Bybee 2005).
