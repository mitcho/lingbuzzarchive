(d)e–(d)a: Rethinking the Origins of the Kurdish Imperfective Affixes
Shuan Karim
July 2020
 

This is an early draft of ``Rethinking the Origins of the Kurdish Imperfective Affixes.'' The imperfective aspect in Kurdish varieties is marked by a prefixes, suffixes, preposed enclitics, or combinations thereof. Until this study, the etyma for these markers was unknown. The inability to identify etyma was because early scholars that approached this issue lacked access to the Southern Kurdish data, which points to a unary source for all the diverse formatives, the locative circumposition. The diversity of the formatives results from well-understood semantically motivated changes coupled with regular sound changes and analogy shared by all varieties and contact phenomena, introducing minute variation with exponential consequences. Additionally, the side effects of these changes likely contributed to the rise and fall of ergativity in Southern Kurdish.
