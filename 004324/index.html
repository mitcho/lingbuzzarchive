Attitudinal Objects. Their Ontology and Importance for Philosophy and Natural Language Semantics
Friederike Moltmann
February 2020
 

This paper argues for the philosophical and semantic importance of attitudinal objects, entities such as judgments, claims, beliefs, demands, and desires, as an ontological category fundamentally different from that of events and states and from that of propositions. The paper clarifies the relation of attitudinal objects to Davidsonian events, critically discusses Twardowski's distinction between actions and products, and argues for a semantics of attitude reports based on attitudinal objects.
