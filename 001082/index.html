Explaining and Unifying Optional Wh-Movement
Norman Yeo
July 2010
 

This paper seeks to propose that optional wh-movement is, contrary to popular belief, compatible to current Minimalist syntax. Despite the fact that the Minimalist Programme eschews optionality, studies have shown numerous wh-movement languages such as French, English, Portuguese, Greek, Spanish and more display grammatical non-echo in-situ options. The claim in this paper is that question particles are far more prevalent in languages than previously assumed to be, and these particles take wh-phrases as their complements forming a larger constituent. Optionality arises naturally by the satisfaction of the EPP feature on C by raising the wh-phrase, the particle or the wh-phrase/particle constituent. Such an approach allows us to explain the wh-optionality of several of the above languages and makes predictions about scope assignment and appears to also account for Left Branch Extraction cases.
