The Syntax-C/I Interface: Functional Discrepancies between CLLD and ‘Bare Left Dislocation’ in Modern Greek
Axiotis Kechagias
October 2011
 

Only in CLLD the dislocated DP performs the discourse function [Topic] partitioning the utterance into [Topic] + [Comment]; cliticless non-focal dislocatedDPs are solely fronted [Ground] information that partition the utterance into[Ground] + [New]. In other words, the two constructions correspond to two different information packaging strategies and grammaticalise two distinct Information Structure interface rules.
