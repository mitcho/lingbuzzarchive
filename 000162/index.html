Pronouns Postpose at PF
David Adger
April 2005
 

It's a well known but curious fact that weak object pronouns move righwards in Irish and Scottish Gaelic. This squib argues, on the basis of the interaction between ellipsis and this pronoun postposing phenomenon, that the latter must take place in the PF component, rather than being a syntactic operation involving remnant movement.
