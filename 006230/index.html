Unconditional Questions are Suppositional and Independent Issues
Yurie Hara
October 2021
 

This paper offers a framework that combines inquisitive semantics with Kaufmann’s (2000) stack-based semantics of conditionals to analyze (un)conditional statements and questions. In particular, the analysis demonstrates how unconditional questions are dynamically interpreted and explains why some speakers find the construction illicit regardless of contexts as well as why dependence between the antecedent and consequent issues deteriorates the acceptability of the construction. The analysis also supports for the suppositional semantics of conditionals. The stack-based account of (un)conditionals allows us to maintain intuitive interpretation procedures of (un)conditionals and simple semantics of answers to (un)conditional questions.
