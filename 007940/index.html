Unmarked and distantly assigned case in ATB movement
Arthur Mateos
October 2023
 

Across-the-board (ATB) movement is known to exhibit case-matching requirements that are alleviated by syncretism. This paper examines two kinds of case in the context of ATB movement: Unmarked case, and case whose assignment relies on an element external to the conjunction site (''distantly assigned case''). I show that unmarked case and distantly assigned case obey the same case-syncretism requirements as other kinds of case, which is unexpected under existing multidominance-based sharing analyses of ATB movement. I present a derivational pathway for sharing structures in which material comes to be shared upon merger of two structures containing the same syntactic element from the numeration.
