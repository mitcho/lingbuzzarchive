Alternatives, density, and in-adverbials
Vincent Rouillard
August 2020
 

Temporal in-adverbials such as "in three days" combine with perfective telic predicates and specify their runtime. We discuss in this paper the fact that the truth-conditions of negative sentences with in-adverbials are not complementary to those of their positive counterparts. We show how assuming sentences containing in-adverbials obligatorily compete with a set of formal alternatives defined in terms of a dense scale can accurately predict these non-complementary truth-conditions.
