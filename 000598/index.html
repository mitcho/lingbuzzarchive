Antisymmetry and the Lexicon
Richard Kayne
February 2008
 

The noun-verb distinction may not be a primitive property of the language faculty, but may rather be underlain by antisymmetry/antioptionality. The execution of this idea as developed here leads to the characterization of nouns as having neither complements nor specifiers. That in turn leads to the conclusion that 'the fact that...', derived nominals, and sentential complementation are varieties of relative clause structures.
