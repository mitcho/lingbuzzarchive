Focus and wh in Jamaican Creole: Movement and exhaustiveness
Stephanie Durrleman, Ur Shlonsky
June 2012
 

Wh & ex-situ focus constructions in Creoles have been analysed in terms of movement to the left periphery (Saramaccan: Aboh (2006), Jamaican: Durrleman (2008), Papiamentu: Kouwenberg & Lefebvre (2007)) and as clefts (Haitian: Lumsden & Lefebvre (1990) Manfredi (1993), Jamaican: Patrick (2007), Veenstra (2008)). Our goal is to reassert the cartographically-rooted left-peripheral account, as in Rizzi (1997; 2004a) and related work.
