The Principle A Problem
Pritty Patel-Grosz
March 2014
 

This paper explores the possibility of a uniform approach for the examples in (1) which are sensitive to Principle A of the Binding Theory and the examples in (2), which are not sensitive to Principle A of the Binding Theory: (1) a. John saw himself (in the mirror) b. * John knows that Maria saw himself (on the reality show) (2) a. John saw his pathetic self (in the mirror) b. John knows that Maria saw his pathetic self (on the reality show) In order to explain the distribution of (1) and (2), I propose that the lexical entry for self denotes a relation R, which I assume is equivalent to something along the lines of PART-OF. I argue that this part-of relation can pick out different aspects of an individual, and that the difference between modified and unmodified self arises due to a requirement at the syntax-semantics interface.
