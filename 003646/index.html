On Conditions for the Duplication of the Japanese Politeness Marker -Mas-
Keisuke Yoshimoto
August 2017
 

Miyagawa (2012) notes that the Japanese politeness marker -mas- is a genuine Main Clause Phenomenon occurring only in main clauses, and that it is licensed by agreement within Speech Act Phrase in the topmost CP phrase of a sentence. However, it has been observed that -mas- can appear in a subset of subordinate clauses if there is also -mas- in the main clause (Uchibori 2007, 2008). However, even if an embedded clause is transparent enough to allow the -mas- duplication, the embedded -mas- is not allowed to occur under certain conditions. I argue that the embedded -mas- is blocked in a clause which involves operator movement.
