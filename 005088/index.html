Extended Phase Boundaries and the Spell-out Trap
Ken Safir
March 2020
 

Grano and Lasnik (2018) (henceforth G&L) argue that phases should be extended if features are still unvalued on the complement of a phase head at the end of what would normally be a phase. Thus if pronouns are bound variables with unvalued features, then the phase that matters for resolving the unvalued features is extended. Without challenging their empirical claims about all the constructions they extend their generalization to, we argue that one phenomenon they regard as consistent with the BPE is not part of it. However, the one case that their generalization should not cover, namely, local anaphora, suggests that phase extension based on unvalued features is not the right explanation of the BPE and that phases for anaphora are not coordinated with phases that restrict the relations that the BPE encompasses.
