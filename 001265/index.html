Three Agreement Alternations in Dutch and their Interactions
Peter Ackema, Ad Neeleman
May 2011
 

In Dutch, there are three factors that trigger alternations in subject-verb agreement: subject-verb inversion, verb class, and politeness. For example, second person agreement is omitted under subject-verb inversion, unless the subject pronoun is marked as polite. We try to figure out how the system works, providing evidence for feature geometry, impoverishment rules and a restriction on impoverishment that we call the ban on accidental deletion.
