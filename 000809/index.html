Coordination, Dependency, and Gapping in Japanese at the Interfaces
Yosuke Sato
January 2009
 

Gapping in Japanese is best analyzed by the coordinate and dependent ellipsis theory proposed by Williams (1997). This analysis provides a natural explanation for several syntactic, semantic, and phonological properties associated with this construction that would remain unexplained under previous analyses resorting to Right-Node-Raising, LF Copy, PF-String Deletion, Syntactic Movement + PF Deletion, and Argument Ellipsis. Our proposed analysis has right theoretical characteristics to accommodate the multi-modular aspects of Japanese gapping in a unified fashion to the degree that previous uni-modular analyses cannot. The payoff of our present analysis, of course, is that it achieves this result from independently motivated principles of coordination, dependency and their manifestations at the interfaces among syntax, phonology, semantics, and processing.
