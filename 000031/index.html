What's in a name? Syntactic and asyntactic accentuation in Dutch.
Jan-Wouter Zwart
February 2003
 

Discusses a stress shift pattern of Dutch family names and place names derived from syntactic phrases. The proposal is that names are taken to be syntactically atomic, and subject to phonological stress rules. Conversely, the stress pattern of phrases must be a function of structure, i.e. of the structure building process Merge.
