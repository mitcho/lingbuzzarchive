Multiple Spell-Out and Contraction at the Syntax-Phonology Interface n
Yosuke Sato
January 2008
 

This paper explores the nature of the syntax-phonology interface from a recent derivational model of syntax with a case study of contraction. Specifically, it is claimed that the Multiple Spell-Out Model of syntax proposed by Chomsky (2000, 2001, 2004, 2005) and Uriagereka (1999) allows for a straightforward characterization of the set of environments within which wanna-contraction, auxiliary reduction, and pronominal cliticization may apply. The proposed analysis achieves this result by allowing a sharply constrained interaction of the syntax with the external interpretive components through a restricted range of information including the Spelled-Out domain finality and the vP edge for the purposes of phonological and semantic interpretation. Particular details of the proposed analysis provide support for various well-motivated principles of syntax such as the economy of projection and the notion of vP/CP phase. The present analysis, therefore, achieves the minimalist goal of explaining linguistic phenomena solely from interface conditions.
