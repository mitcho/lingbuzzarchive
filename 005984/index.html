Complex left periphery in A'ingae
Maksymilian Dąbkowski
May 2021
 

I propose that second position clitics in A'ingae are C-heads of matrix clauses. A'ingae (or Cofán, ISO 639-3: con) is an Amazonian isolate spoken by the Cofán people in northeast Ecuador and southern Colombia. The language is endangered and severely underdocumented. A'ingae has relatively free word order in matrix clauses, discontinuous constituency, and extensive pro-drop. Thus, it shows the typical hallmarks of a non-configurational language. Nevertheless, I argue that A’ingae has a configurational CP projection, which can be diagnosed by second-position (P2) clitics.
