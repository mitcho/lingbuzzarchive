On dative subjects and agreement with infinitives licensed by an external P head (NELS 52 proceedings)
Irina Burukina
May 2022
 

//For a detailed discussion of PPs in Mari see Burukina (2023): https://ling.auf.net/lingbuzz/007081.// The paper focuses on the behavior of infinitival rationale clauses in Meadow Mari (Uralic; head-final, SOV) that allow overt subjects and agreement on the infinitive. I propose that the presence of both dative subjects and agreement marking depends on the presence of a postpositional head Pdat that takes some infinitival clauses as its complement. Pdat can exceptionally assign dative case to the embedded subject; AGR also becomes available due to the properties of the dative adposition and does not result from a relation established between the subject and the embedded T head. The Mari data contribute to the discussion of overt subjects and agreement outside of the finite domain across the worldâ€™s languages. The proposed analysis opens a way for comparison of Mari with other typologically distinct non-Uralic languages that allow exceptional case licensing by a preposition heading the embedded clause.
