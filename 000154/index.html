Locative Inversion in English
Hans Broekhuis
December 2005
 

This article aims at reformulating in more current terms Hoekstra and Mulder's (1990) analysis of the Locative Inversion (LI) construction. The new proposal is crucially based on the assumption that Small Clause predicates agree with their external argument in phi-features, which may be morphologically reflected, as in the case of adjectival agreement in Italian copular construction Maria e malata (3sg.fem), or abstract, as in the case of the corresponding English copular construction. Given this assumption, Hoekstra and Mulder's analysis can be rephrased in terms of the following hypothesis: If A and B agree in phi-features, both A and B can be the goal of some higher head H with uninterpretable/unvalued phi-features, and, consequently, be a candidate for internal merge with H. This actually constitutes the null hypothesis from the perspective of the Last Resort condition on movement: given that A and B share the same phi-features they can both serve in checking (valuing or deleting) the phi-features of the attracting head H.
