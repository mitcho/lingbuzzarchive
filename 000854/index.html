Syntactic Properties of Numeral Classifiers in Mandarin Chinese
Niina Ning Zhang
April 2009
 

This study aims to investigate the syntactic representations of numeral classifiers in Mandarin Chinese, which function as noun-sorters, mass-dividers, and counting units semantically. Analyzing certain newly revealed generalizations, we claim that the dependency between a numeral and a Cl is syntactic as well as semantic, that not all Cls are able to divide mass and not all syntactic positions may a Cl function as a counting unit, and that various types of Cls have different syntactic positions and thus the so-called ClP is not justified.
