A Note on English else
Richard Kayne
August 2021
 

All instances of English 'else' must be accompanied by a noun. This leads to the postulation of silent THING (or PLACE or REASON) in a range of cases. The noun requirement on 'else' may be a subcase of a more general requirement that all arguments must be built on a noun, whether pronounced or unpronounced.
