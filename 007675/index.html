Generative Computational Modelling
Paola Merlo, Giuseppe Samo
October 2023
 

Computational modelling and simulation-based research are becoming an accepted, and in some cases dominant, way of doing science. Simulations and computational modelling are a third way to science, complementing experiments and theory. These methods have become commonplace in many scientific disciplines, and in some areas of the language sciences. We present several types of models. Conceptually, they can be organised by the amount of manual intervention in the construction of the model: some models define both attributes and values by hand; some models predefine the attributes but estimate the values by corpus-driven data collection; some models define both attributes and values by data-driven induction.
