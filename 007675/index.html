Generative Computational Modelling
Paola Merlo, Giuseppe Samo
October 2023
 

Computational modelling and simulation-based research have become an accepted way of doing science, next to experiments and theory. We present several types of models to analyse and compare theoretical proposals. The models in the chapter are organised in a progression of the designerâ€™s intervention in the definition of the model and, correspondingly, of the induction of both the model structure and the values of the models parameters: from defining both attributes and values, to estimating the values by corpus-driven data collection, to models defined entirely by data-driven induction. We argue that far from being the purely statistically-driven models, these increasingly complex language models provide us with increasingly complex mechanistic explanations of the induction of internal I-language representations from externalised and estensional E-xpressions of language, and, therefore, cast light on how fundamental properties of language can be learnt, and how grammars can be organised.
