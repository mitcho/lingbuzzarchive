Linguistic Representations: a note on terminology vs. ontology
David Adger
May 2019
 

This brief, rather philosophical, note explains how generative syntacticians typically use the word `representation' to mean `structure', with no intent that anything is actually represented. It contrasts this view with an alternative due to Georges Rey, who argues that linguistic representations actually represent non-existent objects, so they are intentional. I argue instead that linguistic structures get their connection to the world through standard processes of transduction and computation, so there is no need to involve intentionality.
