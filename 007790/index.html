CV-alternations in Polish without segmental co-occurrence
Jelena Stojkovic
May 2023
 

Palatalisation in Polish is known to produce different results depending on the morpho-syntactic context: while the {\sc loc} marker is shaped simultaneously by palatalisation, vowel dissimilation and vowel retraction, and the {\sc ins} marker avoids any interaction with the stem, the SRs of both are blurred by surface palatalisation (SP; Rubach 1984). \ I argue against direct reference to segmental co-occurrence constraints by treating the exceptionality of the {\sc loc} and {\sc ins} forms as an inherent property of the suffixes (Inkelas et al. 1997), in the form of an abstract UR. The SRs of these case forms are derived by fusion (Struijke 2002) and sharing (Hayes 1986) of features in the phonological domain. The empirical advantage of this proposal is that it predicts the C-V interaction on the basis of the morpheme involved: an abstract UR induces a change on the stem because of over- or underspecification, whereas a concrete UR does not because of its well-formedness. The prediction about Slavic palatalisation in general is confirmed, as no Slavic language shows identical results for PCP and SP, if the latter is present at all.
