3am interview 'Parts, Wholes, Abstract Objects, Tropes, and Ontology'
Friederike Moltmann
September 2024
 

This 2014 interview illuminates various aspects of my work at the time on natural language ontology. The focus was (1) on the new kind of part-whole structure that I had argued is reflected in the semantics of natural language (a notion of part structure based on a notion of integrated whole (conditions of form, boundary, connectedness etc.) and not just a part-of relation) and (2) the importance of the category of tropes (particularized properties) in the ontology of natural language (natural language shows pervasive reference to tropes and reference to abstract objects only in a periphery). The content is just as relevant today, when notions of integrated whole (maximal connectedness) and tropes have now become quite common in particular semantic analyses.
