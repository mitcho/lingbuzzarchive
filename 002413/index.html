Long-distance genitive of negation in Lithuanian
Peter Arkadiev
February 2015
 

It is shown that in Lithuanian negation can trigger the replacement of the accusative case marking of the direct object by the genitive not only locally but also across a potentially unbounded number of infinitival clauses. This phenomenon is investigated from the point of view of verbs that allow it and possible blocking factors, on the basis of both elicited and corpus data. Areal parallels in Slavic and Baltic Finnic languages are discussed, too.
