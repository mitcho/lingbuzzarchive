English it seems has hyperraising
Daniel Greeson
April 2023
 

In this handout, I argue that English does in fact have raising out of a finite clause (i.e. hyperraising), contra current consensus (Zyman 2023). I argue that classic hyperraising sentences like (a) `*Mei seems is happy' are ruled out by the theta-criterion. However, when a quasi-referential `it' is merged to absorb the P(ERCEPTUAL)-SOURCE theta-role assigned by CP-embedding 'seems,' the resulting sentence is grammatical: (b) `Mei it seems is happy'. I prevent a series of novel data to show that the highest DP in (b) sentences (e.g. `Mei') indeed satisfies classic diagnostics of A-movement and that these sentences can't be simply reduced to a parenthetical or A'-topic analysis. In light of my findings, I revisit classic conditions on A-movement like the PIC, Activity, and Ban on Improper Movement. Feedback extremely welcome!
