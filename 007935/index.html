Modes, Disturbances, and Spatio-Temporal Location
Friederike Moltmann
March 2024
 

This is a paper in descriptive metaphysics, arguing that modes (tropes) and disturbances (folds, flaws, scratches) fail to have a direct location in space, a location not mediated through their bearers. But the paper also gives a novel, ontological account of Chomsky's fly-flaw examples: 'There is a fly believed to be in the bottle' vs. the unacceptable 'There is a flaw believed to be in the argument', thus showing that the Chomskyan contrast is straightforwardly explained in ontological terms and provides an argument in favor, rather than against, referentialist semantics.
