Unifying Anti-Agreement and Wh-Agreement
Nico Baier
December 2016
 

I argue that 'wh-agreement', in which dedicated agreement morphology cross-references an A'-extracted argument, and 'anti-agreement', in which an A'-extracted argument cannot control full agreement, are two instantiations of the same abstract phenomenon. Both effects are are the result of a φ-probe copying both φ- and wh-features from a goal. Patterns of anti-agreement and wh-agreement arise when partial or total impoverishment applies to the [φ+wh] feature bundle in the morphological component, blocking insertion of an otherwise appropriate, more highly specified agreement exponent.
