Sprouting and the structure of except-phrases
Richard Stockwell, Deborah Wong
January 2021
 

This paper investigates the structure of except-phrases based on sentences like (1), where the why-sprout is ambiguous (cf. Rudin 2019: ex. 39): (1) Sam introduced nobody to Mary, except John, but I don’t know why. a. ...Sam introduced nobody to Mary, except John. (People usually like Mary.) b. ...Sam introduced John to Mary. (John rarely gets along with people.) We argue that the except-phrase in (1) contains elided clausal structure, which provides the required syntactic antecedent for the 'John’ reading (b). When an except-phrase can be shown not to contain clausal structure, the ‘John’ reading disappears. This pattern supports the view that ellipsis requires a syntactic antecedent, and reveals diversity in the syntax of except-phrases.
