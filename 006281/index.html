Modal movement licensed by focus
Ka-Fai Yip, Tommy Tsz-Ming Lee
August 2021
 

[Published in https://www.jbe-platform.com/content/books/9789027258175-la.272.06yip] This paper investigates the distribution of root modals in Chinese, and their interaction with focus interpretations. We address a long-standing puzzle concerning the position of root modals: A-not-A questions appear to exceptionally license the otherwise unavailable pre-subject position for root modals. Upon closer examination, we observe that (i) A-not-A formation does not always license the pre-subject position of root modals and (ii) that there are environments other than A-not-A formation that also license the position. We propose that root modals can undergo movement across a focused element to a higher position, and this movement is constrained by a version of output economy.
