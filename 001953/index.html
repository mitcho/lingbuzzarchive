SUMMING QUANTITIES OF OBJECTS AT THE LEFT EDGE
Niina Zhang
November 2013
 

This paper analyzes the syntactic structure of the construction represented by How many books did Ken borrow and Kim steal in total?, in which the semantics of the left-edge nominal (e.g., how many books) is cumulatively “shared” by the objects of the two clausal conjuncts. Some efforts have been made to analyze the semantics of the construction in the literature. However, syntactically, the construction has been understudied and considered a real challenge to generative grammar. Extending Chaves’s (2012 ‘Conjunction, cumulation and respectively readings’, Journal of Linguistics 48:297-344) analysis for a subject-sharing construction, I propose a predicate-forming analysis for the object-sharing construction. While providing a possible syntactic derivation for the construction, the research also probes the properties of the specific type of A-bar movement.
