“English 'One' and 'Ones' as Complex Determiners”
Richard Kayne
June 2015
 

'One' and 'ones' are complex determiners whose relation to their antecedent, when they have one, is mediated by a silent noun. They are never themselves nouns taking an antecedent directly. All non-local syntactic relations necessarily involve internal merge.
