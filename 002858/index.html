The Unicity of 'There' and the Definiteness Effect
Richard Kayne
January 2016
 

If we take the identity in form between expletive 'there' and various other instances of 'there' (not only locative 'there', but also deictic 'there' and the 'there' of 'thereby') not to be accidental, we are led to the conclusion that expletive 'there' originates DP-internally as an instance of deictic 'there' (as in non-standard 'that there book') and that the definiteness effect plays out entirely DP-internally as a conflict between deictic 'there' and certain determiners.
