Third person-sensitive allomorphy of accusative case of possessed nominals in Balkar and Kumyk
Daniar Kasenov
December 2022
 

Theories that posit the privative nature of φ-features are known to struggle with cases of morphological sensitivity to third person to the exclusion of other persons. This paper discusses a case of such sensitivity in accusative case allomorphy in Balkar and Kumyk where the accusative case affix has a special form for third person possessives only. This pattern is argued to be made possible by a syntactic decomposition of feature sets: if one assumes that bundles of privative features are built by the syntax, then the sensitivity of allomorphy to third person exclusively can be derived via locality condition on allomorphy, which is not met when PART and SPKR feature-nodes intervene.
