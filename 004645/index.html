E-type pronouns: congressmen, sheep and paychecks
Rick Nouwen
June 2019
 

This handbook chapter discusses properties of and account for e-type pronouns. It first takes the presence of maximal reference and covariation as two dimensions that can be used to characterize e-type phenomena and it sketches the dynamic semantic approach that deals with such properties in the literature. The chapter also compares the dynamic approach to views that treat pronouns as descriptions (of some kind).
