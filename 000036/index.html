One more time
Michael Brody
June 2000
 

Hornstein (1998) argued like Brody (1995) that a theory with both chain and move is redundant, one of them should be eliminated. He presented two arguments that the concept of A-chains should not be part of the grammar. In my comments (Brody 1999) I showed that the arguments for this way of eliminating the redundancy are flawed and that their background is dubious. Hornstein in his reply (2000) disagrees, but as set out below, without valid argument.
