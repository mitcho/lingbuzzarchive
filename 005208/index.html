Comparatives bring a degree-based NPI licenser
Linmin Zhang
May 2020
 

Comparatives license the use of negative polarity items (NPIs) within their than-clause. What exactly constitutes the NPI licenser in comparatives? In this paper, I argue that it is the very status of being the standard in a comparison that constitutes the NPI licenser. Based on Zhang and Ling (2020)â€™s interval-subtraction-based theory on comparatives, I show that by serving as the standard in a comparison and playing the role of subtrahend in a subtraction equation, a than-clause is inherently downward-entailing. Moreover, it demonstrates strong negativity like the classical negation operator not. Therefore, a than-clause licenses both weak and strong NPIs. Crucially, this NPI licenser is due to monotonicity projection based on degree semantics (implemented with intervals), not due to a set-operation-based negation operator.
