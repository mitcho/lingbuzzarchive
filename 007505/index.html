Defocus leads to syntax-prosody mismatches in right-dislocated structures
Ka-Fai Yip, Xuetong Yuan
August 2023
 

While focus is long recognized to lead to syntax-phonology mismatches by triggering re-organization of prosodic domains (Truckenbrodt 1995, i.a.), this rephrasing view has been recently challenged by an alternative view where focus does not trigger rephrasing (Féry 2013, Ishihara 2016). This paper contributes to this debate by probing into an under-appreciated aspect of the issue: whether the lack of focus (Defocus), instead of the presence of focus, may lead to syntax-prosody mismatches. We investigate a variant of right-dislocated structures, dislocation copying (DC) in Cantonese and Mandarin, both of which do not involve focus-rephrasing effects. We show that while DC has a biclausal structure syntactically (2 CPs), it consists of only one intonational phrase prosodically (1 ι). We argue that this mismatch is due to defocus in the right-dislocated chunks where focus interpretation is systematically banned. We propose that defocus cannot receive head prominence and leads to an illegitimate headless intonational phrase, and hence triggers the rephrasing. This proposal represents a defocus rephrasing view that weakens the rephrasing view by dissociating the role of defocus in prosody from that of focus.
