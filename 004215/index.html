Forbidden clitic clusters in Zapotec
Steven Foley, Nick Kalivoda, Maziar Toosarvandani
September 2018
 

We examine clitic clusters in several Zapotec varieties (Oto-Manguean: Oaxaca), which have some familiar restrictions. First, they exhibit Gender–Case Constraints (GCCs): certain combinations of third-person subject and object clitics are not permitted depending on their position on a four-way gender hierarchy. Second, these Zapotec varieties do not allow certain combinations of identical third-person clitics, which we will call a *X–X Constraint. The repairs for these constraints fail to conform to the predictions the Division of Labor, according to which the violation of a morphological constraint can only be repaired through a morphological operation and, conversely, the violation of a syntactic constraint through syntactic means. (Rezac 2011, Nevins 2011). For both GCCs and the *X–X Constraint, the repair is periphrasis: a strong pronoun replaces the object clitic. Yet, the *X–X Constraint clearly has a morphological source.
