A Critical Evaluation of Greenberg's Classification of Arawan, Chapacuran, and Muran: When Not Saying Anything is Best
Daniel Everett
December 2012
 

This paper was originally written for the 1990 conference on the peopling of the Americas at the University of Colorado. I am making it publicly available for the first time here because of many questions that have arisen over the years over the genetic classification of the Piraha language. It examines the Greenberg classification of Piraha as Macro-Chibchan and rejects it, claiming that, modulo the now-extinct languages of the Muran family, Piraha is a language isolate.
