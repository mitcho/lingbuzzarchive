Relabeling and Multiple Spell-Out: Raising-to-Object as Epiphenomenon
Yosuke Sato
March 2010
 

This squib suggests that the so-called raising-to-object operation, long known since Postal (1974) and recently resurrected in the minimalist analysis (Lasnik and Saito 1991; Koizumi 1995; Lasnik 1999), does not exist in grammar at least in the way it has been treated in the literature (i.e. via movement). It is an epiphenomenal consequence of two independently available mechanisms in natural language syntax: underdetermined labeling (Chomsky 2008) and multiple spell-out (Uriagereka 1999).
