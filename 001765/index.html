Biolinguistic Platonism remains an Oxymoron
Christina Behme
August 2014
 

This paper argues that Watumull (2013) fails to unify the ontologies of abstract linguistic objects and concrete biological brains because he [1] seriously misunderstands fundamental points of the Katz & Postal criticism and [2] conflates ontological issues in very much the same way that Noam Chomsky does. I conclude that six decades after the Chomskyan revolution, the foundations of biolinguistics remain internally incoherent.
