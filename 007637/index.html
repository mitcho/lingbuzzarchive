The Semantics and Pragmatics of Multi-Head Comparatives
Linmin Zhang
November 2023
 

This study addresses the semantics and pragmatics of English multi-head comparatives such as "Less land produces more corn than before" (von Stechow 1984). With regard to semantic derivation, I argue that a multi-head comparative is similar to a cumulative-reading sentence (e.g., "exactly 3 boys saw exactly 5 movies", see Brasoveanu 2013) and involves a split between (i) the introduction of discourse referents (e.g., items that are land or corn) and (ii) a sentence-level maximization and measurement. With regard to pragmatics, I argue that such a multi-head comparative addresses a correlation between two measurements, which in turn addresses an underlying degree question-of-discussion (QUD), i.e., how much has the corn productivity rate increased? I will also address how the phenomenon of multi-head comparatives sheds light on the notion of informativeness.
