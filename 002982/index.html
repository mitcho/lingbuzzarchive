Integrated Parentheticals in Quotation and Free Indirect Discourse
Alessandra Giorgi
May 2016
 

In this work I consider the properties of two kinds of parentheticals, those introducing Quotations and Free Indirect Discourse: (1) I will leave tomorrow, said John (2) The new ration did not start till tomorrow and he had only four cigarettes left, thought Winston (adapted, from Orwell, 1984) In example (1) the parenthetical is said John. Example (2) is a FID construction and the parenthetical is thought Winston. These two kinds of parentheticals have peculiar properties from an interpretive, syntactic and phonological point of view. I propose here an integrated view of these parentheticals, in the spirit of Kayneâ€™s (1994) Linear Correspondence Axiom and briefly discuss possible extensions to other cases.
