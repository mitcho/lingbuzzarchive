Covert partial wh-movement and the nature of derivations
Hadas Kotek
June 2015
 

Wh-movement is commonly thought to be caused by a syntactic probing operation, initiated by an interrogative probe on C, and triggering subsequent movement to the specifier of C. In this paper I argue that at least covert wh-movement cannot be described in these terms. I argue instead that covert movement can optionally target positions other than interrogative C, and that movement is triggered by the interpretational needs of the wh-phrase itself. Evidence will come from the interaction of English multiple wh-questions with intervention effects: I document a pattern of intervention effects that is explained only if English in-situ wh-phrases are interpreted neither in their base-generated positions nor at interrogative C, but in non-interrogative intermediate positions.
