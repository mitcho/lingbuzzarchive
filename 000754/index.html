Toward an Analysis of French Hyper-Complex Inversion
Richard Kayne, Jean-Yves Pollock
September 2008
 

French hyper-complex inversion, like its better-known counterpart complex inversion, is an instance of clitic doubling that lends itself to a complex DP analysis. Such an analysis, combined with an analysis of French interrogative '-t-', yields an account of a number of salient properties of these inversion constructions. Whether an equally satisfactory account could be achieved via Chomskys Agree needs to be looked into, as does the converse question whether a complex DP analysis of ordinary verbal agreement could replace one based on Agree.
