On expletive, semantically void, and absent subjects
Hubert Haider
July 2019
 

The paper offers empirical and theoretical reasons for an essential, but widely neglected, differentiation of the subjects of impersonal constructions. The essential difference is the syntactic difference between a semantically empty subject argument on the one hand and an expletive item in an obligatory subject position. A structurally expletive subject is an SVO phenomenon. In the SOV clause structure, arguably, there is no obligatory subject position and therefore no room for expletive subjects. SOV and VSO languages allow for genuinely subjectless clauses, for structural reasons; an [S[VO]] clause structure does not. Expletives need to be distinguished from semantically void but lexically selected subject pronouns. They must not be null. In the linguistic reality, there is no such thing as an "empty expletive", contrary to assumptions in the literature. This is – as shall be demonstrated – a grammatical concept without any factual basis. It merely betrays a strong but empirically unsubstantiated SVO bias.
