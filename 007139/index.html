Compositional semantics of same, different, total
Oleg Kiselyov
June 2015
 

We propose a strictly compositional and uniform treatment for the internal readings of the symmetric predicates such as `same' and `different' and for the summative phrases such as `a total of'. The analyses handle multiple occurrences of symmetric and summative predicates in the same sentence, alongside of multiple quantificational elements. We treat symmetric predicates and summative phrases as wide-scope generalized existential quantifiers for choice functions. Like Barker's parasitic scope, we treat symmetric and summative expressions as generalized quantifiers and relate them to universal quantification. However, our quantifiers scope wide rather than parasitically and avoid Barker's non-standard interpretation of universal quantification. We introduce the analyses in terms of the familiar Quantifier Raising framework, which we make more precise and treat as a somewhat informal version of a type-logical grammar.
