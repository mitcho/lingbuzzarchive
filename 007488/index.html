Anti-Locality Explains the Restricted Interaction of Subjects and Parasitic Gaps (WCCFL 41 proceedings)
Colin Davis
August 2023
 

This paper examines the interaction between parasitic gaps and subjects. This is a multifaceted topic which previous literature has discussed in a scattered fashion. I integrate a variety of facts which I argue reveal that parasitic gaps and subjects interact productively, except when interrupted by anti-locality - a ban on certain movements that are illegally short. I argue that such anti-locality predicts when parasitic gaps in subject position are allowed, when subject movement licenses parasitic gaps, and has implications for the distribution of subject A-bar movement in general.
