Diachronic formation of Chinese cleft (是-的) constructions: 'lateral' grammaticalization revised
Keith Tse
March 2018
 

Simpson and Wu (2002) propose that the formation of Mandarin Chinese cleft (是-的) constructions, namely the two variants where de（ 的）is either sentence-final (VOde) or verbal suffixal (VdeO), consists of a form of 'lateral' grammaticalization where de（的）is reanalysed from a nominal category (D) to an equivalent clausal category (T(past)). Recent analysis has disproven the chronology of Simpson and Wu's analysis, as VOde and VdeO seem to have evolved independently with little correlation (Han (2012), Zhan (2012), Long and Xiao (2009, 2011), Long (2013)), but a formal analysis of the historical data reveals that 'lateral' grammaticalization can still be maintained for Chinese cleft constructions as de（的）does undergo categorial reanalysis from a nominal category to a structurally parallel clausal category in copular constructions headed by copula shi（是）, which not only clarifies the formation and typology of Chinese cleft constructions but also provides a more nuanced definition of 'lateral' grammaticalization.
