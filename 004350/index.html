Non-verbal predication in Bantu
Patricia Schneider-Zioga
December 2018
 

This is a handbook article that provides an overview of non-verbal predication in Bantu languages. The study of non-verbal predication in Bantu languages is particularly inviting as Bantu languages employ a rich variety of invariant particles, verbal copulas, null copulas, directly inflected non-verbal predicates, and tone to establish non-verbal predication, as examined in this overview. A predication asymmetry in terms of person (1st & 2nd versus 3rd person subjects) is noted and explored. Also addressed is a taxonomic question: what do Bantu languages tell us about how many types of copular clauses there are? A syntax-semantic interface question is explored: what do Bantu languages tell us about how many copulas there are from the perspective of meaning? Secondary predication in Bantu languages, including small clauses and depictives, is also investigated.
