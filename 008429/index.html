Stress Location and the Acquisition of Morpho- Syntactic Parameters
Hisao Tokizaki
February 2011
 

In this paper, I argue that the canonical stress position determines the possibility of compounding and preposition stranding in a language. Based on an analysis of Stowel (1981), Snyder (2001) and Sugisaki and Snyder (2002) argue that it is the compounding parameter that decides compounding and preposition stranding. However, the nature of the parameter is not is not discussed in detail, and it is not clear how the parameter is set in language acquisition. I argue that the compounding parameter itself is not necessary in grammar by showing how the parameter is derived from the stress position in a language.
