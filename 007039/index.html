The phonetics and phonology of secondary articulations in Abkhaz
Bert Vaux
May 1997
 

Abkhaz employs as part of its rich consonantal inventory two secondary articulations, palatalization and labialization. Each of these secondary articulations behaves as a natural class with respect to rules and constraints of Abkhaz phonology, yet each also has a number of different phonetic and phonological manifestations. The purpose of this paper is to present the basic phonetic and phonological properties of these secondary articulations, and to develop a theory of representations for these articulations that captures their underlying unity and surface diversity. The data are drawn from my fieldwork with Zihni Pəsiypa, one of the last speakers of the previously unstudied C°əž´ə dialect of Abkhaz.
