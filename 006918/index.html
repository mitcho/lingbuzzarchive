Whales, and other mammals: a Naturalist's take on syntactic variation
Nigel Duffield
November 2022
 

This paper presents an intentionally anachronistic view of universal constraints on phrase-structure that many will regard as superficial, and out of touch with Minimalist developments; nevertheless, it may be of some interest to others, especially those interested in Vietnamese or Modern Irish. Its publication here is prompted by the appearance of Collins (2022, Biolinguistics]), whose radically autonomous ideas concerning phrase-structure and its acquisition by children are irreconcilably opposed to the thesis presented here, namely, that (for the most part) "syntax makes sense". This is the published version of a presentation originally given in April 2021 at an online workshop hosted by the Harvard Yenching Institute, entitled ‘Vietnamese Linguistics, Typology and Language Universals.’
