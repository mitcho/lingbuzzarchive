Stress location and comparative forms in English
Hisao Tokizaki
May 2013
 

Word-stress location in English has been discussed in terms of extrametricality in rule-based phonology (Liberman and Prince 1977, Hayes 1982), or in terms of the NonFinality constraint in Optimal Theory (Prince and Smolensky 1993). In this paper, I will investigate the nature of English stress from a morphosyntactic and typological point of view. I will focus on a process at the interface between phonology and morphosyntax, that is, comparative forms in the languages of the world. In section 2, I will overview the two comparative forms in English, i.e. morphological comparative and periphrastic comparative. Section 3 classifies languages of the world into three types according to their comparative forms. In section 4, I show that this classification corresponds to the word-stress location in the languages. Section 5 is devoted to explaining why word-stress location correlates to the choice of comparative forms.
