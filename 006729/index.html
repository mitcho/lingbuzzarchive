Grammatical constructions and rhetorical figures: The case of chiasmus
Randy Harris
January 2020
 

Many rhetorical figures are constructions in the contemporary sense of Construction Grammar, form/function pairs, and many of them are in fact hiding in plain sight, participating in common constructions, sometimes in clusters. This paper argues that Construction Grammar offers the perfect framework to continue the rhetorical turn begun in Cognitive Linguistics by the incorporation of metaphorical and metonymical features; in fact, we might call this a rhetorical return, to pre-Enlightenment approaches to language in which rhetoric and grammar were mutually informing disciplines. It makes this argument by way of an extended illustration of a construction with high figural density, the A out of B but not B out of A Construction ("You can take a boy out of the country, but you can't take the country out of a boy").
