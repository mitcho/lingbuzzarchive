Some Remarks on Agreement and on Heavy-NP Shift
Richard Kayne
May 2003
 

A case is discussed in which movement to subject position takes place in English, but in which the verb agrees with a different argument than the subject. This is argued to involve object agreement with the verb and simultaneous subject agreement with an unpronounced auxiliary. A link is also proposed between Heavy-NP-Shift and the position of definite articles, namely that if a VO language has a DP-final definite article, then it lacks heavy-NP shift.
