The Late Insertion of Germanic Inflection
Uli Sauerland
July 1997
 

In this paper, I will provide evidence that syntactic heads are lled with phonological content after the application of syntactic transformations. The question of whether syntax precedes or follows morphology in a derivational grammar divides morphological theories into two classes: lexicalist theories assume that morphology precedes syntax; late insertion theories assume the opposite order. In principle hybrid theories assuming late insertion in some case, early insertion in others would also be possible, but the two extreme views are simplest and hence conceptually preferred. Therefore, arguments against one of the extremes, are arguments in favor of the other extreme. I provide two arguments again st the pure version of the lexicalist theory in sections 1 and 2. In section 3, I will present an argument in favor of late insertion.
