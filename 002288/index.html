Incorporation (handbook article)
Jason D. Haugen
November 2014
 

This handbook article overviews two distinct senses of the term "incorporation". The first refers to a word-formation process akin to compounding, and is typically applied to the particular noun-verb compounding process known as "noun incorporation". The second refers to a syntactic approach to word-formation innovated by Mark C. Baker, head-movement, which was proposed to account for noun incorporation and other verb derivations such as causatives and applicatives. Competing theoretical approaches to noun incorporation are discussed and evaluated, and then the application of Baker’s incorporation approach to other derivational contexts, especially deadjectival and denominal verbs, is discussed and assessed.
