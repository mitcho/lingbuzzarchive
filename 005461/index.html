Unary/Binary-NEG Structures of NPIs and Reduplication in Buli
George Akanlig-Pare, Ken Hiraiwa
September 2019
 

Negative-sensitive items in Buli, a Mabia (Gur) language spoken in Ghana, exhibit a mixed behavior between NCIs and NPIs. Thus, Buli presents a counterexample to Vallduví’s generalization. Adopting and extending the framework of Collins & Postal (2014) and Collins et al. (2017), we will show that the apparently mysterious mixed behavior of negative-sensitive items in Bùlì can be explained by articulating an unary-NEG structure and syntax of reduplication.
