Aspectual operators: Temporality, evaluativity, and polarity sensitivity
Teodora Mihoc
August 2022
 

The aspectual operator "still" features interesting properties related to temporality, evaluativity, and polarity sensitivity. This is not unique to "still" but rather true of aspectual operators more generally—very similar properties can be found in "already", "anymore", and "yet". And it is not unique to aspectual operators either—very similar facts have been reported for disjunction, indefinites, minimizers, or numerals. This paper starts from Beck (2020)’s proposal for temporality in "still"; generalizes it to "already", "anymore", and "yet"; and then generalizes it even further to capture evaluativity and, partially, polarity sensitivity also, drawing on Mihoc (2021)’s proposal for similar properties in superlative-modified numerals, which in turn draws on previous insights about disjunction, indefinites, minimizers, and other numerals. The result is a new solution to temporality, evaluativity and, partially, polarity sensitivity in still that unifies it not just with other aspectual operators but also with disjunction, indefinites, minimizers, and numerals.
