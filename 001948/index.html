Degree comparisons across possible worlds: Measure phrase modification with '-(i)na'
Taehoon Hendrik Kim
March 2014
 

In this paper, I argue that the Korean particle '-(i)na' is a presupposition trigger that probes into a VP meaning to draw a comparison between two degrees, one from the actual world and the other from the speakerâ€™s expectations. Syntactically, I posit that '-(i)na' is a verbal Degree head in the extended VP.
