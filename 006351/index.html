The Distribution of Consonants and Vowels in Disyllabic Base Word Patterns N8 (v-V) and N9(V-v) in Hawaiian
Nina Pilszczikowa-Chodak
December 2021
 

This article analizes the distribution of consonants and vowels in words of Patterns N8(short/long vowel - 91 words) and N9(long/short vowel - 15 words) of the so called Nine Hawaiian Nucleei Patterns. The usage of vowels after obstruents and sonorants is examined. Significant absences of examples are noted. Of interest is the prevalence of words with initial obstruents /',h,k,p/ in Pattern N8. The role of the feature [grave] is examined.
