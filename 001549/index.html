On Different Analyses of Additive and Prepositional Numerals
Carmen Florina Savu
May 2012
 

In this paper I elaborate on numeral-noun constructions with prepositional and additive numerals, drawing mainly on data from English, Romanian, Macedonian and Russian. Two analyses are presented and I attempt to argue that it may be reasonable to assume that prepositional numerals are phrasal constituents, both in English examples like around ten, and in Romanian examples like treisprezece. As for additive numerals, the English and Romanian data seem to point to the conclusion that they are best viewed as constituents, while the Macedonian and Russian data appear to suggest that they mightbe better analyzed as in Ionin and Matushansky (2006.)
