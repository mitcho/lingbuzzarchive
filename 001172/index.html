On the Syntax of Raising Verbs
Diego Krivochen
May 2011
 

(Written in English) In this paper we analyze the phenomenon of raising-to-subject. After a very brief introduction to the topic, we deal with two main problems: the underlying Relational Semantic Structure (Mateu, 2000, 2002, 2008) and its relevance to syntax, and the "narrow syntax" of raising. In the end, we propose a radically minimalist theory of raising in which we claim that we only have one clause with a Split TP instead of what has been traditionaly proposed, the presence of two clauses and an extraction from a subordinate TP or phi-domain (Chomsky, 1999, Hornstein, 2003, Grohmann, 2003, 2004). Comments will be welcome.
