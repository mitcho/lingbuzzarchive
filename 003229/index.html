The Dative Case in the Malayalam Verb
Karattuparambil Jayaseelan
July 2013
 

The main claim of this paper is that every Malayalam verb contains an incorporated Dative case; this claim is supported by morphological evidence. The more general claim for linguistic theory is that the Verb is a non-primitive lexical category which is derived when a nominal predicate “absorbs” the Dative case of the first argument that it merges with. This can be seen as an extension of the claim about the genesis of Adjective made in Amritavalli & Jayaseelan (2003). An ancillary idea is that the three cases, Genitive, Accusative and Dative, are generated in that order on every argument of a predicate, with Genitive as the innermost and Dative as the outermost case; and Accusative case is “revealed” when verb formation absorbs the first argument’s Dative case.
