Displaced and misplaced genitives
Josef Bayer
January 2005
 

Although this has rarely been noticed, German shows extraposition of adnominal genitives. A syntactic account of this process in terms of the LCA requires leftward movement and subsequent topicalization of the remnant. However, leftward movement of genitives is strictly impossible in the languages. Remnant movement would therefore amount to some kind of repair movement. An alternative is suggested by which extraposition is the result of prosodic rephrasing in the sense of Truckenbrodt (1995), an operation which leaves core syntactic properties unaffected.
