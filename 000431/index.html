Not "only I": Notes on the Syntax of Focus Binding
Tom Roeper
April 2006
 

This paper examines the famous sentence from Heim "Only I got a question I could answer" from a syntactic point of view, building a more sophisticated version of barriers and relativized minimality. The crucial observation is that all forms allow this kind of binding,including Proper Names, as in: Only Mary still looks like Mary in these old pictures [entails: John doesn't look like John in these old pictures notL John doesn't look like Mary in these old pictures[
