Possessives Consist of Heads and Complements: Some Notes
Dorian Roehrs
May 2010
 

This paper argues that German(ic) Possessor Doubling Constructions such as Peter sein Auto (Peter his =) Peters car are Possessive Phrases, consisting of a possessive head sein his and its complement Peter, the Possessor. It is proposed that PossP is base-generated in the theta domain of the nominal head. If left in situ, Norwegian bilen hans Per car-the his Peter obtains; if moved, German Peter sein Auto Peter his car is brought about where the Possessor has moved to a higher position within PossP.
