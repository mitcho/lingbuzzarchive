Morphology and syntax in the Scandinavian vernaculars of Ovansiljan
Piotr Garbacz
August 2012
 

The paper discusses two hypotheses that link the presence of the so-called ‘rich verbal agreement’ with occurrence of a number of syntactic patterns. The first approach discussed is the one presented in Bobaljik & Höskuldur Thráinsson (1998) and the second one proposed by Holmberg (2010a). The present paper confronts both hypotheses with new data from a group of Nordic vernaculars spoken in western Sweden (Ovansiljan). It demonstrates that the approaches cannot account for the presented data in a satisfactory way. Hence, this paper argues in favour of abandoning the link between morphology and syntax, as formulated in the above-mentioned works.
