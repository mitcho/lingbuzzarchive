The Post-Syntactic Morphology of the Albanian Pre-Posed Article: Evidence for Distributed Morphology
Jochen Trommer
July 1999
 

The so-called "preposed article" (PA) is a functional morpheme that plays a central role in the morphosyntax of Albanian DPs but is poorly understood theoretically. In this paper I present an analysis of PAs morphology along the lines of Distributed Morphology (DM; Halle and Marantz, 1993). PA is taken to be a post-syntactically inserted agreement morpheme. It is argued that a correct account of the insertion contexts of PA and of its allomorphy presupposes reference to surface syntactic configurations thus supporting the conceptual framework of DM. The claim that PA is in certain instances a constitutive element of word formation (cf. e.g. Buchholz and Fiedler, 1987) is rejected.
