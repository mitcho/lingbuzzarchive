Oops, I Did It Again: A Typology of Phonological Iterativity
Samuel Andersson, Bridget Samuels, Bert Vaux, Kozzy Voudouris
March 2021
 

This paper provides a typology of iterativity in phonological processes. Previous work often recognizes only simultaneous (Chomsky and Halle 1968) or only iterative application (Kaplan 2008). We conduct a typological survey showing that that both iterative and simultaneous processes must be represented in the grammar. While previous literature largely focuses on obligatory processes, we show that the distinction between iterativity and simultaneity is easiest to identify under optionality. We propose an analysis using Search & Copy, a framework for phonological computation with both simultaneous and iterative implementations. The full typology identified in our survey can be accommodated with a minor modification of the Directionality parameter Î´, which we suggest has the three settings {Left-to-Right, Right-to-Left, Simultaneous}.
