Subject and non-subject ex-situ focus in Dagbani
Sam Issah, Peter W. Smith
September 2018
 

This paper offers a description and account of the patterns of ex-situ focus in Dagbani. We show that there are two syntactic strategies for creating ex-situ focus in the language, one involving A’-movement to the left periphery, and the second involving base generation in the left periphery combined with coreference to a resumptive pronoun. Furthermore, we argue that subjects are difficult to move from Spec,TP to Spec,FocP in the left-periphery because of anti-locality, which creates a tension when trying to focus subject, which are required to derivationally fill the specifier of both positions. We further show that what looks to be a two- way distinction between the behaviour of subjects and non-subjects in the language is in fact a three-way distinction between matrix subjects, embedded subjects and non-subjects. Finally, we situate our account of Dagbani within the wider context of other languages in the West African region, showing similar restrictions on subject movement, but different strategies to resolve the tension.
