Asymmetry in Emphatic Topicalization
Josef Bayer
January 2001
 

This article explores a type of movement to the specifier of a dependent CP in Bavarian  distinct from wh-movement  which is described as emphatic topicalization (ET). Once ET has applied to a CP, this CP itself is required to undergo topicalization. It is argued that ET invokes a force feature in CP that can only be interpreted if the CP has access to the force projection of the matrix clause. ET differs from Left Dislocation as it can involve genuine quantifiers and can license parasitic gaps.
