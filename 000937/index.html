Probing knowledge of similarity through puns
Shigeto Kawahara
October 2008
 

This paper outlines the aims, results and future prospects of a general research program which investigates knowledge of similarity through the investigation of Japanese imperfect puns (dajare). I argue that speakers attempt to maximize the similarity between corresponding segments in composing puns, just as in phonology where speakers maximize the similarity between, for example, inputs and outputs. In this sense, we ﬁnd non-trivial parallels between phonology and pun patterns. I further argue that we can take advantage of these parallels, and use puns to investigate our linguistic knowledge of similarity. To develop these arguments, I start with an overview of the results of some recent pro jects, and follow that with patterns that provide interesting lines of future research. I hope that this paper stimulates further research in this area, which is much understudied in the linguistic literature.
