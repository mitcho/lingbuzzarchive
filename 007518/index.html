What phonology is and why it should be
Alex Chabot
September 2023
 

This chapter is a discussion of the epistemology of phonology. It proposes an overview of phonology as a theory in the tradition of scientific realism, a rationalist investigation of a property of the human mind. The chapter is an effort to locate phonology within human cognition, as an information-processing capacity, a part of the innate Faculty of Language that allows for humans to mediate between the infinite physical world and the mind. It traces the development of phonology from the end of the 19th century through today, arguing that, while phonological knowledge is correlated to substantive facts in the physical world, it does not reproduce them. This has important consequences for how phonological theories should be constructed and how phonological analysis should be carried out.
