Pseudo-incorporation vs. Differential Argument Marking in Korean
Imke Driemel, Hyunjung Lee
October 2022
 

Previous corpus work on optional case marking of Korean arguments (Ha. Lee 2006, 2008; Kwon and Zribi-Hertz 2006, 2008) has identified challenges to an analysis in terms of markedness scales (Aissen 2003). While case loss on indefinite bare nouns display interpretive effects familiar to pseudo-noun incorporation, more prominent categories scoring higher on the definiteness scale can also undergo case loss with no such effects. In this paper, we will provide a detailed case study based on scope, binding and control tests, showing that lack of case-marking is indicative but does not entail pseudo-noun incorporation in Korean. The data, however, can still be captured with an Aissen-style markedness scale, if the scale differentiates between DPs and NPs. Thus, a scale-based account can be naturally combined with a DP/NP account of pseudo-noun incorporation, where NPs constitute the lowest member of the definiteness scale (see also von Heusinger 2008). We conclude from the results that pseudo-noun incorporation and differential objetc marking are separate phenomena: whereas the former relates case loss to a size difference and thus triggers semantic effects, the latter simply relates case loss to the position on the definiteness scale.
