On the syntax of multiple sluicing
Klaus Abels, Veneeta Dayal
January 2017
 

We provide an explanation of the pervasive generalization that multiple sluicing obeys a clausemate condition by appealing to properties of covert movement. Additional wh-phrases in multiple sluicing undergo covert wh-movement, made overt by ellipsis. This wh-movement is clause bounded and subject to superiority. Together, these assumptions explain the clausemateness condition on multiple sluicing. We motivate clause boundedness and sensitivity to superiority of covert wh-movement independently.
