A Problem For/Of Tough Movement
Randall Hendrick
October 2013
 

This paper aims to further our understanding of complements tough movement predicates like 'tough', 'easy' and 'pleasant'. It is argued that, when these adjectives head phrases that are complete propositions semantically, they should constitute phases, a syntactic domain that is transferred for phonological processing and unavailable to subsequent syntactic operations. Corroboration for this claim is gleaned from the contrasting interaction of tough-movement with 'of' and 'for' phrases
