A Note on Roots and Redundancy
Jason D. Haugen
January 2009
 

This squiblet points out an interpretational asymmetry between "cognate object" and "cognate subject" constructions. Whereas root identity between a denominal verb and its direct object results in pragmatic redundancy (cp. ?Hortense danced a dance. vs. Hortense danced a jig.), root identity between a verb and its agentive subject does not (cf. The snitch snitched). I suggest that this asymmetry is a novel argument in support of Hale and Keyser's syntactic incorporation account of English denominal verbs.
