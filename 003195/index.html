Taming the Hungarian (in)transitivity zoo. Undiagnosed species and a complete derivation of the morphosyntactic patterns.
Andrea Márkus
July 2015
 

In my PhD thesis, I put forward an account of the intricate morphosyntactic pattern displayed by the causative-inchoative alternation and some related construction(s). Inchoatives, causatives, and two overlooked, but closely connected Hungarian constructions, the 2DPC and the half-passive, are shown to line up along an (in)transitivity scale, and the constructions with varying degrees of (in)transitivity are brought under a unified analysis on the premises of progressively growing syntactic structures. The assumption that morphemes have an internal syntactic structure allows me to capture morphological diversity, speaker variation and a puzzling morphology vs. syntax/semantics mismatch in an elaborate morphosyntactic account.
