A note on pharyngeal features
Bert Vaux
January 1999
 

In this paper I propose that (i) [atr] is not an ersatz feature employed to encode height distinctions, as has been claimed by Clements 1989, but rather plays an important role in both consonants and vowels; (ii) the distribution of uvulars, both in terms of alternations with velars and the general lack of the voiced uvular stop G, is motivated by the nature of the feature [atr]; (iii) all properties of guttural (i.e. lower-vocal-tract) segments observed by the researchers mentioned above can be captured by the feature geometry proposed in this paper; and (iv) the tongue root features [atr] and [rtr] are analogous to tongue height features in producing a three-way contrast in possible segments.
