Unifying the that-trace and anti-that-trace effects
Jamie Douglas
December 2015
 

This article proposes a unified analysis of the that-trace and anti-that-trace effects in English. Unification of these two seemingly diametrically opposed effects remains an outstanding problem in the literature. Assuming that CP can be split or not and an antilocality condition, a novel analysis is set out consisting of a number of postulates that have theoretical implications for phase theory, the mechanics of successive cyclicity, and the distinction between final and intermediate landing sites.
