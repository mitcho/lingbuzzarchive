Pronominal Licensing in Mam
Tessa Scott
October 2020
 

Person Case Constraint (PCC) effects have been tied to subject/object person restrictions (Boeckx 2000, Béjar and Rezac 2003). Two dialects of Mam (Mayan) exhibit a restriction on 3rd person subjects with local person objects (Ixtahuacán, England 1983; Cajolá, Pérez Vail 2014). In this paper, I argue that the Mam person restriction should be analyzed in the same way as the PCC. However, Mam presents a puzzle for many theories that analyze transitive person restrictions as PCC violations because the two arguments are in the opposite hierarchical order relative to the probe. While many Agree-based theories of the PCC predict the opposite pattern, Deal’s (2020) theory is able to account for the Mam pattern. To account for the full data, I additionally proprose a pronominal licensing condition which stats that all pronouns must be agreed with by an Local Person (LP) probe. Evidence for this comes from the antipassive repair for person-violating structures. In the antipassive repair, each pronoun is in the domain of its own LP probe.
