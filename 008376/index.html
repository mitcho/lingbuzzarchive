Русские предикативы и онтология состояний [Russian predicatives and the ontology of states]
Anton Zimmerling
August 2024
 

I argue that the Russian dative-predicative construction has the meaning of internal D-state, i.e. spatiotemporal stative situation with a priority argument. Most predicatives licensing dative-predicative structures in Russian also express external D-state, if used without overt dative subjects. This can be proved both physical sensations, cf. X-y kholodno ‘X is cold’ vs kholodno ‘It is cold’ and for some affections, cf. tosklivo ‘dreary’, ‘sad’, Х-у tosklivo ‘Х feels sad’ vs zdes’ tosklivo ‘It’s dreary here’. If a lexical item has regular uses in the dative-predicative structure, it generally can express the meaning of external state outside this structure. The reverse if false: if a lexical item has regular uses as an external state, cf. vetreno ‘windy’, pyl’no ‘dusty’, it only can have infrequent uses with a dative subject. I check an additional list of words with the meaning of external state, measure their frequencies in the context “predicative + dative subject in 1Sg” and compare them to standard dative predicatives.
