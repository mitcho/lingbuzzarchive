Book Review on Elly van Gelderen (ed), Cyclical Change Continued. Linguistik Aktuell/Linguistics Today Volume 227. Amsterdam: John Benjamins, 2016. Pp. 429.
Keith Tse
March 2020
 

Elly van Gelderen's book Cyclical Change Continued (2016) contains seminal, cutting-edge ideas on issues to do with formal syntax, Minimalism, historical linguistics and grammaticalization. This book is ambitious in range and scope as it builds on van Gelderen's own work on formal syntax and grammaticalization with a rich collection of typological data and presents a new formal account on traditional debates regarding linguistic cycles and typology, namely the seemingly cyclic transition between analytic and synthetic structures in the history of languages. However, while the book is empirically impressive, there are controversies surrounding analyticity and syntheticity in historical syntax that are only briefly acknowledged (Ledgeway (2012:10-29)), a more detailed analysis of which could significantly improve our understanding of language typology through the perspective of grammaticalization.
