Some consequences of Categorial Deficiency for the Theory of Grammar
Phoevos Panagiotidis
April 2005
 

This paper capitalises on Categorial Deficiency, the idea that functional elements are specified for uninterpretable categorial features, to derive a number of little studied characteristics of phrase structure. Hence, it reduces the inability of functional heads to appear without a complement and the non-occurrence of lexical heads in the middle of a projection (unless there is a second lexical head at the bottom thereof) to the feature-checking requirement of such uninterpretable categorial features. Furthermore, it proposes that the most common type of Head Movement can be construed as a subcase of the Agree relation holding between interpretable categorial features of lexical heads and the uninterpretable ones of functional heads, while the consequences of the above for the theory of Agree are examined. Finally, it employs Categorial Deficiency in order to propose an endocentric, Bare Phrase Structure compatible analysis of mixed projections, where nominal and verbal projections coexist, as the result of a switch head mediating between the verbal and the nominal part.
