Does Armenian have glottalised IOR ejective stops?
Bert Vaux
November 2022
 

Impressionistic observations by Eduard Sievers in 1876 of “Kehlkopfverschluss” in the speech of a young Armenian visitor from Tbilisi initiated a snowball of assertions over the next 145 years that the series of consonants in Modern Armenian traditionally labelled “plain voiceless stops” are actually glottalised or ejective as in numerous other languages of the Caucasus. This claim has in turn fuelled a number of larger debates about the nature of the Proto-Indo-European consonant system, the age of glottalisation within the attested 1500 year history of Armenian, the phonetic criteria for identifying glottalisation and ejectivity, the merits of ear vs instrumental assessment, etc. In this talk I review the claims that have been made about the synchronic and diachronic status of glottalisation in Armenian varieties, evaluate the sorts of phonetic evidence that have been presented, and consider what phonetic methods might be best suited for working under properly controlled experimental conditions with the surviving speakers of the varieties claimed to have glottalisation.
