Generative Approaches to Noun Incorporation
Michael Barrie
March 2015
 

This article outlines the history of generative approaches to noun incorporation, giving a current state of the art description of the phenomenon and its analysis. I also discuss the future outlook of this phenomenon and why it is important to linguistic theory in general. Note: This is a chapter in a co-authored book. For bibliographic purposes it is considered a multiply co-authored book, while in practice it is an edited volume.
