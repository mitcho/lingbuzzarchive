Does relativized minimality account for wh-island amelioration?
Emily Atkinson, Aaron Apple, Kyle Rawlins, Akira Omaki
April 2015
 

This paper reports four acceptability judgment experiments that investigate the amelioration of wh-island violations, with a special focus on the role of D-linked wh-phrases. These observations have played a prominent role in theories of islands, such as a recent version of Relativized Minimality that attributes amelioration effects to the distinctness of the formal feature set on the moved constituent and intervener (Rizzi 2013). We show that the distribution of D-linking amelioration effect is not consistent with Featural Relativized Minimalityâ€™s predictions, and argue that wh-island amelioration effects are better explained by semantic distinctness of the two wh-phrases.
