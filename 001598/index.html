Structural Signature of Pronouns
Mark Baltin
August 2012
 

This paper argues that pronouns (more generally, pro-forms) are simply represented by the structural configurations into which they enter- a functional head whose complement is deleted. This view of pro-forms explains why, if traces are viewed as original copies, these originals can convert to pronouns without violating inclusiveness, and is a more satisfying account of how pro-forms are represented in a symbolic cognitive architecture than a feature [+pronominal] (or, generalized, [+pro-form]), and accounts for the similarities between pronouns and deletion sites that have led some to take deletion sites to be pro-forms. This analysis, however, argues the reverse- that pro-forms involve deletion, and that rather than assimilating deletion to pro-forms, deletion is basic.
