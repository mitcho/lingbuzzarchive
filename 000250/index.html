Does Scrambling in Japanese Obey the Coordinate Structure Constraint?
Shuichi Yatabe
May 2003
 

In this paper, I demonstrate (i) that the initial conjunct of a coordinate structure can be scrambled out of that coordinate structure, (ii) that a non-initial conjunct of a coordinate structure cannot be scrambled out of that coordinate structure, and (iii) that a part of a conjunct cannot be scrambled out of that conjunct. These three observations do not make sense in the standard, movement-based theory of scrambling (Harada (1977); Saito (1985)). On the other hand, they receive a natural explanation in the linearization-based theory of long-distance scrambling, proposed in Yatabe (1996).
