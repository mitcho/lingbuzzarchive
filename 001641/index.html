CVC Roots in Hebrew
Sasson Margaliot
October 2012
 

In this article I argue that the Hebrew tri-consonantal root spells out the composite Syntactic Object formed by Merge of an elementary CVC root and one or more functional heads. I also present five novel rules that allow us to find the CVC root for many Hebrew words. These five formal rules make no use of informal semantic intuitions, but rather are precise and algorithmic. The striking support for this proposal derives from the simple fact that these five rules are all in complete agreement with each other.
