Salvation and non-salvation of defectiveness under ellipsis
Gesoel Mendes, Andrew Ira Nevins
October 2019
 

In this squib, we discuss cases of salvation and non-salvation by deletion in the domain of defective verbs. We distinguish two types of defective verbs: (i) defective verbs that can be saved by PF-deletion, which we take to lack an eligible allomorph for certain environments within a language, and (ii) defective verbs that cannot be saved by PF-deletion, which we take to signal the lack of a morpheme necessary to build certain structures within a language. Ellipsis thus arises as a reliable tool to probe into lexical representations.
