Unifying Phrasal and Word-Internal Ordering: The Final-Over-Final Condition in Superlative Adjectives and Beyond
Colin Davis
August 2023
 

I observe and analyze a generalization about morpheme order in adjectives. In some languages, superlative adjectives are derived from comparative ones and thus contain what would in isolation be a comparative affix, as Bobaljik (2012) shows in detail. I argue that the cross-linguistically attested orders of comparative and superlative morphemes in such forms obey the Final-over-Final Condition (Sheehan et al 2017), which states that a head-initial phrase cannot be dominated by a head-final phrase. Research on the FOFC focuses on phrase-level syntax. However, we also expect the FOFC to apply to word-internal morpheme order, if theories like Distributed Morphology are right that morphology realizes the output of syntactic structure building. I argue that morpheme ordering in synthetic superlative adjectives verifies this prediction.
