Phrasal layering versus complex heads in Greek stative passives
Lefteris Paparounas
May 2023
 

Many syntactic approaches to stative passives employ phrasal layering: under these anal­yses, stative passives effectively have a phrasal verbal substructure that can be as big as that of eventive passives, from which they are differentiated only by a stativizing layer. Greek has been taken to support such analyses, owing to the observation that agent-­oriented modi­fiers seem to occur freely in stative passives formed with the stativizer –men–. This paper identifies a novel set of generalizations suggestive of wide­-ranging differences between stative and eventive passives in Greek. I argue that these generalizations provide evidence against phrasal layering, supporting instead a complex head approach to stative passives, without any phrasal structure below the stativizer.
