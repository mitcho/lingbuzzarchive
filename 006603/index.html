From music to dance: the inheritance of semantic inferences
Pritty Patel-Grosz, Jonah Katz, Patrick Georg Grosz, Tejaswinee Kelkar, Alexander Refsum Jensenius
October 2022
 

This paper looks at short musical segments and motion-capture animations of body movements that were generated spontaneously in response to those musical segments. Building on recent research on music semantics, we ask whether abstract meaning inferences that listeners draw on the basis of the musical segments are also inherited by the corresponding body movements. We present an experiment in which participants rate how well the emotion terms Angry, Bored, Calm and Excited are expressed by the auditory stimuli and visual stimuli. The experimental findings indicate a correlation between the sounds and animations with regards to the inferences that participants draw.
