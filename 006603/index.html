From music to dance: the inheritance of semantic inferences
Pritty Patel-Grosz, Jonah Katz, Patrick Georg Grosz, Tejaswinee Kelkar, Alexander Refsum Jensenius
May 2022
 

Music can give rise to abstract semantic inferences about music-external situations. We ask whether dance, defined as music-accompanying body movement for the scope of this paper, also gives rise to similar abstract semantic inferences. We experimentally test whether inferences from a given musical sequence are inherited by body movement triggered by this musical sequence. Our results indicate that such an inheritance of semantic inferences does occur. This finding is consistent with a view where abstract descriptive meaning can be communicated in different modalities, here music and dance.
