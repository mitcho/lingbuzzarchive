Romance Clitics and Feature Asymmetry: An Autosegmental-Based Approach
Louis-H Desouvrey
February 2000
 

This dissertation presents a thorough account of cliticization in three major Romance languages. Relying heavily on a nonlinear representations, as in phonology, I show that a few constraints account for the syntax, the form and the appearance of clitics in French, Spanish, and Italian. The feature-based theory advocated for allows one to explain why personal pronouns in other languages, for instance English, are not clitics, unlike their Romance counterparts.
