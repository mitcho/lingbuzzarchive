Phi-Agree and theta-related Case
Milan Rezac
March 2006
 

The paper examines Basque dialects that differ in transparency of theta-related datives to clausal "absolutive-type" (v) phi-Agree, normally controlled by the structural absolutive (and ergative). Treating the dative as a PP, and thus a phase, explains its normal opacity to external Agree. A phi-probe on the P head of the shell that Agrees with its complement DP renders the latter's phi-features visible to the outside, selectively according to the feature-geometric articulation of the probe. A minimal version of such a probe results in "quirky" case, a theta-related case that is visible to phi-Agree only as 3rd person. The dialects where the dative controls clausal phi-Agree support the hypothesis that theme licensing in double object constructions in such languages involves a second number/gender probe on Appl.
