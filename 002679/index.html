Enclisis/proclis alternations in Romance: allomorphies 1 and (re)ordering
Rita Manzini, Leonardo Savoia
September 2015
 

Romance clitics appear to the left of the verb in I and to the right of the verb in C. This preverbal/postverbal alternation correlates with (a) allomorphy, specifically l- vs. zero (b) stress shifts and (c) internal reordering of the clitic string. In fact, to the extent that modal properties can be associated with negation, the alternations in (a)-(c) are observed between pre-Neg and post-Neg position. In a nutshell, clitic allomorphy and clitic reordering, far from being morphophonological quirks, are seen to externalize syntactico-semantic notions of modality. In dealing with clitic (re)ordering we will further avoid pre-encoding of clitic hierarchies, in favour of a model based on 11 the dissociation between Merge and linear order. In this perspective, phrasal constituents are ordered to the right of the verb in Romance; clitics mirror them in that they are ordered to the left, while keeping the Merge relations constant.
