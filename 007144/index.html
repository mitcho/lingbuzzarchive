On the Adjectivalizer -Si in the Reduplicated and Deverbal Adjectives in Japanese
Kazuya Kudo, Koji Shimamura
January 2023
 

In this paper, we will present a uniform analysis of a Japanese adjective adjectivalzing morpheme -si in the framework of Distributed Morphology (Halle & Marantz 1993). Based on the structure of Japanese adjectives put forth by Nishiyama (1999), we contend that -si in reduplicated and deverbal adjectives is a morphological realization of an adjectival head a (cf. Arad 2003; Marantz 1997; Marantz 2000). Evidence of our analysis comes from the phonological process called Sequential Voicing (SV), or Rendaku (Otsu 1980; Tatsumi 2022), as well as the interpretations of the reduplicated and deverbal adjectives. We also propose that there is another kind of a, which is morphologically null.
