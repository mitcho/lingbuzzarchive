Past participles in Czech: a morphophonological puzzle
Markéta Ziková
December 2021
 

The chapter investigates past participles in Czech. Specifically, I focus on the variation of the shape of the past participle morpheme in the colloquial variety of Czech. The variation poses a morphophonological puzzle: it involves apocope of the past participle suffix -l, which is however not a regular phonological process in colloquial Czech. The puzzle is explained in terms of licensing consonant domains (Scheer 2009, Cyran 2010) which have different parameters in each language variety.
