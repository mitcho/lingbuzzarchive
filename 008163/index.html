On the inventory of v and Voice (NELS 54 proceedings)
Irina Burukina, Maria Polinsky
May 2024
 

While considerable research has been dedicated to the relationship between Voice and v, a number of unresolved questions persist, including (i) the inventory and phasal nature of Voice and v; (ii) their selectional properties, and (iii) positions where the external argument can be introduced. We argue that vP and VoiceP have principally distinct functions and present a comprehensive inventory of v and Voice. Only v can introduce a new thematic relation, while Voice manipulates the pre-existing argument structure, both syntactically and thematically. Consequently, VoiceP is an optional layer added to the structure only when needed. Using data from Kaqchikel (Mayan), we provide empirical evidence for this approach, coming from the distribution of ergative subjects, passivization patterns, morphological causatives, and “vacuous causatives”, which we analyze as an instance of applicativization.
