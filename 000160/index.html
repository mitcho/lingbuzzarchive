Determiner heads as arguments and the Pronominal Argument (macro)parameter
Phoevos Panagiotidis
February 2004
 

This paper looks into Jelineks Pronominal Argument Parameter and argues it not to predict the existence of languages taking both affixal and phrasal arguments, such as Koranic Arabic, Basso Polesano as well as Greek, Romance and many others. It then goes on to propose an analysis whereby in (at least) these languages all arguments are Determiner heads and that their surface appearance is a result of these Determiner heads own selectional properties.
