Compounding in Distributed Morphology
Heidi Harley
February 2008
 

This article proposes an account within the framework of Distributed Morphology for English compounding, including synthetic compounds, root (primary) compounds, and phrasal compounds. First a summary of the framework is provided. Then, an analysis is proposed according to which compounds are incorporation structures, where non-head nouns incorporate into the acategorial root of the head noun, prior to its own incorporation into its category-defining nÂ° head.
