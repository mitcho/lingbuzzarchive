The unavailability of temporal de re in English infinitives
Deniz Satik, Susi Wurmbrand
July 2022
 

This paper provides novel evidence for the view that infinitives are deficient in tense when compared to finite clauses. Based on the results of an experiment which was conducted via Prolific with 600 native speakers of English, we show that speakers have a clear preference for finite complements over nonfinite ones when a de re interpretation is intended. This leads us to claim that infinitival tense mirrors the obligatory de se property of infinitival subjects: it is well-known observation that infinitival subjects such as PRO need to be read de se. Our conclusion only follows if infinitives lack tense but is not expected if tense is present.
