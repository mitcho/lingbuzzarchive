Highs and Lows in English Attachment
Nino Grillo, Joao Costa, Andrea Santi
June 2015
 

Grillo and Costa (2014) claim that Relative-Clause attachment ambiguity resolution is largely determined by whether or not a Pseudo-Relative (PR) interpretation is available. PR-availability, however, covaries with the semantics of the main predicate (e.g., perceptual vs. stative). Experiment 1 assesses whether this predicate distinction alone can account for prior attachment results by testing a non-PR language, English. We found low attachment independent of predicate type, though predicate type had a minor modulatory role. Experiment 2 shows that English, traditionally classified as Low Attachment, can demonstrate High Attachment in the presence of a globally ambiguous Small-Clause/(reduced) Relative-Clause environment. These results support a grammatical account of previous effects and provide novel evidence for the parser's preference of a Small-Clause over a Restrictive interpretation, cross-linguistically.
