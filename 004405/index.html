Prepositions as Probes
Richard Kayne
May 2001
 

Certain prepositions are not merged with what we think of as their objects. Rather, such prepositions enter the derivation outside VP and subsequent to merger of a phonetically unrealized Case-licensing functional head. That functional head acts as a probe attracting to its Spec what we think of as the object of the preposition. The preposition itself is a probe with respect to VP, which moves to Spec of the preposition, producing the desired word order.
