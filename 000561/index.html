Determination Parameters in the Semitic Diglossia
Abdelkader Fassi Fehri
November 2006
 

The contribution investigates salient variation in the Semitic determiner domain from synchronic and diachronic perspectives. It builds on comparative work on N-to-D movement, associating D with its referential content. It is claimed that properties of overt determination make use of two interpretable features on D: Individuation and Person. Individuated vocatives are shown to be double bares. The variation provides grounds for developing a computationally based parametric historical treatment.
