Nguni bare nouns: licensing without Case
Vicki Carstens
June 2023
 

This paper explains the distribution of Nguni augmentless nominals in terms of their properties as strict negative dependents and wh-words: they require a licensing relation with an operator NEG or CQ, and they have focus features. In addition, since any nominal can become a strict negative dependent by dropping its augment, I propose that all augmentless nominals have uNeg features which can interfere with labeling when they occupy preverbal subject positions. These factors suffice to account for restrictions on [-A] nominal distribution without reference to abstract Case, contra Halpert (2015), Pietraszko (2021). In the revised versions I have presented a modified account of the permeability of subjunctives, and corrected various minor errors.
