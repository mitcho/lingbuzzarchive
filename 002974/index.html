Resumptive Prolepsis A study in indirect A'-dependencies
Martin Salzmann
May 2006
 

In this thesis, I argue in favor of the matching analysis of relative clauses. The argument is largely based on German data showing that there is systematic absence of Principle C effects even if reconstruction is forced otherwise. The matching analysis also plays an important role in the analysis of prolepsis as found in long-distance relativization in German and Dutch (the book of which I believe that John likes it). I propose that the complement clause is turned into a predicate by operator movement. After combining with the matrix verb, this licenses an additional constituent, the proleptic object. Proleptic object and operator are related to each other by means of ellipsis, accounting for systematic mismatches. In the last part of the book, I address Swiss German relative clauses, arguing for a base-generation analysis.
