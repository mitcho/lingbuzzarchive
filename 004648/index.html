Fake Indexicals and their sensitivity to focus
Itai Bassi
June 2019
 

I present a novel analysis of fake indexicals in focus constructions ("only I did my homework") and in relative clauses ("I am the only one who did my homework"), and new data that supports it. The analysis implies that phi-agreement between binders and bindees results from a syntactic dependency that applies at the derivation to LF, and not through a separate PF operation. It makes use of the special binding mechanism proposed originally in Kratzer 1991 to encode focus dependencies more generally ("Tanglewood" sentences). The new data, from English and Hebrew, shows that fake indexicals in relative clauses without "only" are sensitive to the presence of contrastive focus, which poses a challenge to current "minimal pronoun" approaches to pronominal binding (Kratzer 2009, Wurmbrand 2017). The cross-linguistic variation in this domain is also briefly discussed.
