Exfoliation: towards a derivational theory of clause size
David Pesetsky
February 2019
 

I argue that properties of infinitival constructions in languages like English that are commonly taken to follow from case theory actually fall together with a broader generalization about reduced clauses and have nothing to do with nominal licensing. Instead, I propose a 21st-century revival of an idea from the earliest days of generative grammar: that infinitival clauses are derivationally created from clauses that start their lives full and finite. The proposal makes immediate predictions about clauses that are reduced in size, but remain finite, yielding a new account of complementizer-trace effects and other contrasts. This is very much a draft, posted to solicit comments.
