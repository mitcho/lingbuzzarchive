The syntax of the Finnish question particle
Anders Holmberg
January 2008
 

The Finnish yes/no-question particle ko (or kö) is typically (en)cliticized to the first constituent of the sentence. In unmarked questions it is cliticized to the fronted finite verb or auxiliary, which has been described as head-movement to C, with ko a spell-out of Q-marked C. Questions with a narrowly focused constituent present a problem for this analysis. The narrowly focused constituent is fronted, pied-piping as much as is necessary. The clitic ko can be cliticized to the entire fronted constituent, but can alternatively be cliticized to a subconstituent of it, and not necessarily to the actually focused category. The rule is that the clitic must c-command the focused category. The same rule holds for the focus particle kin too, even. The initially appealing idea that the question clitic ko is syntactically equal to the focus clitic kin plus a WH-feature is shown to be empirically untenable. Instead, ko has a more restricted syntactic distribution than kin, always (first-)merging with the maximal DP containing Focus, except optionally below a demonstrative, while kin can merge with any subconstituent of the DP. As focus-particles, what they have in common is that they probe for a Focus-feature. But ko also enters a relation (as a goal) with uWH-marked C, and undergoes wh-movement.
