The Armenian dialect of Salmast
Bert Vaux
May 2022
 

Despite the historical and cultural importance of the Armenian communities of the county of Salmast, their distinctive variety of Modern Armenian remains largely unstudied, limited at best to passing mention as a form of Khoy or Urmia dialect. The dialect moreover is now endangered, with the descendants in the diaspora either not learning the dialect or at best mixing it with features of Standard Armenian and other local varieties. The present monograph addresses these issues by assembling what we know about the linguistic structure of the Salmast dialect, where it was originally spoken and where it is preserved today, how it resembles and differs from other Armenian dialects in the region, and the ways in which it is conservative and innovative relative to Classical, Standard Eastern, and Standard Western Armenian. We focus on phonological features (such as the fronting of following vowels by and subsequent devoicing of original voiced stops, e.g. դու du ‘you’ > տիւ [ty]), morphological features (such as the imperfective in -li ~ -s and analytic past tense formation in -էր -[ɛɾ], e.g. ուզէս էր [uzɛs ɛɾ] ‘he/she/it wanted’), and lexis (e.g. Standard Armenian տալ tal ‘husband’s sister’ ~ Salmast տալվ [tɑlv]; հարսանիք harsanik‘ ‘wedding’ > խառնիս [χɑrnis], զրուցել zruts‘el ‘talk’ > զուռցալ [zurʦʰɑl]), complemented by a parsed text.
