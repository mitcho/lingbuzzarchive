Saussurean Rhapsody: Systematicity and Arbitrariness in Language
Charles Yang
August 2020
 

Psychologically our thought--apart from its expression in words--is only a shapeless and indistinct mass. Philosophers and linguists have always agreed in recognizing that without the help of signs we would be unable to make a clear-cut, consistent distinction between two ideas. Without language, thought is a vague, unchartered nebula. (CLG, p111-112)
