On the syntactic nature of manner-incorporation
Boban Arsenijevic
September 2011
 

The paper argues that, contrary to claims in the literature (Harley 2005), manner incorporation in the derivation of lexical verbs is syntactically driven, just like result and object incorporation is. The arguments used come from the island effects in non-bridging verbs, regularities in the morphosyntactic behavior of Serbo-Croatian verbs at prefixation and cognate results, objects and manner realization in languages like Serbo-Croatiann, Russian, Hebrew or Vietnamese. In a broader perspective, the paper makes a step towards a general view that all lexical verbs are syntactically derived by incorporation of their arguments, and consequently that all lexical verbs involve a complex syntactic structure.
