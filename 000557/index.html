On Deriving Polarity Effects
Antje Lahne
December 2007
 

In this paper, I argue for a new, theory-neutral approach to polarity effects in inflectional paradigms resting upon the idea that the choice of marker for a given feature specification is determined by the choice of marker for a minimally different specification. In paradigms instantiating polarity effects, the matching of morpho-syntactic and phonological features proceeds by natural class based-rules, but is partly overriden by two principles, Discreteness of Environment and Minimality, the latter of which is an independently motivated assumption underlying syntactic derivations, and arguably a basic property of language in general.
