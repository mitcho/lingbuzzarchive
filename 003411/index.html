A morphosyntactic condition on sluicing: evidence from Spanish/German code- switching
Kay-Eduardo Gonzalez, Sergio Ramos
April 2014
 

It is widely accepted that a semantic condition holds for sluicing: the sluice and the antecedent have to entail each other (Merchant 2001). In addition to this, we propose a morphosyntactic condition: the sluice has to share at least some morphosyntactic features with the antecedent. Our data show that the case assigner of the remnant must at least share the case features with its correlate in the antecedent. Our proposal is based on experimental data from Spanish-German code-switching.
