Reflexivity and External Argument Introduction: Insights from Greek
Lefteris Paparounas
January 2024
 

Greek verbal reflexives share their morphology with passives. A long-standing intuition holds that this instance of formal overlap follows from a shared syntax: reflexives look like passives and their ilk because they have the syntax thereof. Though widespread, this intuition has largely remained without direct support, with the argument structure of verbal reflexives having proven particularly difficult to probe using syntactic tests. This paper brings to light a number of novel generalizations supporting the long-standing conjecture: Greek builds verbal reflexives by means of a passive-like syntax, whereby a single syntactic argument originates low. This state of affairs is argued to follow from a view treating (verbal) reflexivity as a type of external argument introduction, supporting the existence of reflexivizing Voice.
