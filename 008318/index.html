A Tiny Tiny Paper: A Null Theory of the Intensive Semantics of Doubled Adjectives
Maxime Papillon
August 2024
 

Adjective repetition is a common way to intensify an adjective, such as in "a tiny tiny bird" meaning "a very tiny bird". Such constructions have previously been analyzed as either a kind of reduplication, or a kind of stylistic repetition. In this article, I argue that the compositional semantics of gradable adjectives already makes "a tiny tiny bird" and "a very tiny bird" synonymous without further assumptions, and I therefore propose that neither reduplication nor stylistic repetition are needed to analyze adjective doubling of the English type.
