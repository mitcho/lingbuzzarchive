The Syntax of Valuation and the Interpretability of Features
David Pesetsky, Esther Torrego
October 2005
 

This paper defends a conception of agreement that draws on various traditions that view it as "feature sharing". We combine this conception with a proposal that valuation and interpretability of features are independent concepts. These ideas taken together allow us to revise existing analyses of a number of syntactic constructions. In particular, we focus on the role of verbal tense morphology in specifying other properties of a sentence, and the comparable role played by wh-morphology in specifying clause type. Particular attention is devoted to the syntax of raising constructions and to an analysis of sentential subjects that improves on earlier work of our own.
