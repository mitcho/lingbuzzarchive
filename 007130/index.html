On Wh and Subject Positions, the EPP, and Contextuality of Syntax
Zeljko Boskovic
February 2023
 

By examining a number of constructions involving local subject A’-movement, the paper argues for a split IP and a contextual approach to the EPP, where the locus of the EPP effect is not a fixed, unique position, but varies depending on the actual syntactic context. It is argued that the landing site of subject wh-movement (who in who left) is lower than non-subject wh-movement (who in who did Mary leave) but higher than regular subjects (Mary in Mary left). Quirky subjects are argued to be lower than regular agreeing subjects, with a generalization proposed regarding their crosslinguistic availability. The overall account is extended to a number of other cases, including Germanic V-2 subjects, languages with subject only relativization strategies, only subjects, Defaka focus movement and Bùlì wh-movement, Norwegian C-marking, Brazilian Portuguese nunca ellipsis, Hong Kong Sign Language rightward wh-movement, overt imperative subjects, clausal subjects, locative inversion, there constructions, Singlish non-agreeing subjects, Japanese and Chinese subjects. Regarding the EPP, the paper argues for a contextual approach to the EPP where, on a par with a contextual approach to phases, there is an EPP domain, with the highest projection in the EPP domain being the locus of the EPP effect (on a par with the highest projection in a phasal domain being a phase). The contextual conception of the EPP is put into a broader perspective regarding a more general theoretical move toward contextuality in syntax, which permeates many domains and which argues against regarding syntax as fully Markovian in nature
