Anti-locality
Norvin Richards
March 2022
 

I review the literature on anti-locality, focusing on proposals by Abels (2003), Grohmann (2003), and Erlewine (2016). I review some of the kinds of phenomena for which anti-locality has been involved as an explanation (including conditions on preposition stranding and on extraction of subjects). I close with a proposal about how to use Contiguity Theory to derive a particular version of Erlewine's (2016) type of anti-locality, the one developed by Brillman (2017), which allows us to derive the distribution and nature of anti-locality effects as a consequence of the particular forces that trigger movement.
