Parallel Work Spaces in Syntax and the Inexistence of Internal Merge
Karattuparambil Jayaseelan
January 2015
 

If we can maintain that the Work Space in which a phrase is built up prior to being initially merged in a derivation does not ‘perish’ with that operation but may be availed of for remerge, we do not need Internal Merge. We can simplify Grammar in other ways too: PIC, the ‘escape hatch’, partial spell out of phrases etc. can be dropped from the theory.
