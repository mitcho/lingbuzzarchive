CAT meets GO: Auxiliary inversion in German verb clusters
Markus Bader, Tanja Schmid
August 2008
 

In this paper we link two phenomena occurring with modal verbs in German: First, verb order variation in 3-verb clusters, and second, the property of modals to appear with non-verbal complements. We present experimental evidence from native speakers of German showing that speakers either accept both the non-standard order V-Aux-Mod in 3-verb clusters and the non-standard order Aux-Mod in 2-verb clusters, or they reject both. Based on these findings, we can identify two grammars---Standard and Colloquial German---with Standard German forming a subset of Colloquial German. To account for these two grammars, we extend the CAT-based analysis of verb cluster formation Williams (2003), and combine it with the empty GO hypothesis of van Riemsdijk (2002). An attractive feature of the analysis proposed here is its broad coverage of the data that derives from slight differences in subcategorization frames of Standard and Colloquial German as well as Swiss German.
