Rule Locality in the Computation of Word Prosody
John Frampton
December 2023
 

The theory of word prosody which is developed in this paper modifies the theory of Idsardi (1992) and Halle and Idsardi (1995) in several ways. Most importantly, it assumes that delimiter insertion rules (DIRs) target beats and insert a delimiter adjacent to the target. This view of DIRs, focused delimiter insertion, allows subrules of the iterative rule, if it is a rule scheme, to compete for application at a particular target. This provides the framework for a conceptually simple and straightforward analysis of ternary stress systems. It also provides the framework for imposing natural locality restrictions on the class of DIRs. These restrictions on DIRs lead to the conclusion that footing must be binary or gapped binary (ternary). Some steps are made towards demonstrating that this restrictive view of DIRs and the footing systems built from them are empirically adequate. The footing systems of Finnish, Chugach, and Tripura Bangla are detailed.
