Co-occurrence of non-article determiners: a window on articulation and bundling in DP structure
Brian Hsu, Saurov Syed
February 2020
 

Although the distribution of articles has played a central role in theories of nominal syntax, the patterning of non-article determiners is considerably less studied. This work examines cross-linguistic variation in co-occurrence among non-article determiners (demonstratives, proper names, pronouns, possessors) and its implications for understanding nominal (DP) structure and its parametric variation. We present data from a range of language families, and show that languages vary in both the number and permitted combinations of non-article determiners within a nominal phrase. These patterns provide new evidence that the primary semantic components of nominal reference, person, deixis, identifiability, inclusiveness (Lyons 1999), correspond to syntactic features in a universal hierarchy: [PERSON]>[DEIXIS]>[INCLUSIVENESS]. Languages vary in how these features are bundled on functional heads; non-article determiners can co-occur only if their corresponding features are on separate heads.
