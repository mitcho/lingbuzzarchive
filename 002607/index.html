Ellipsis, economy and the (non)uniformity of traces
Troy Messick, Gary Thoms
July 2015
 

A number of papers have attempted to account for the interaction between movement and ellipsis in terms of an economy condition MaxElide. We show that the elimination of MaxElide leads to an empirically superior account of these interactions. We show that a number of the core effects attributed to MaxElide can be accounted for with a parallelism condition on ellipsis. The remaining cases are then treated with a generalized economy condition which favors shorter derivations over longer ones. The picture this leads us with is one without the need for the ellipsis-specific economy constraint MaxElide.
