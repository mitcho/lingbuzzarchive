What IS a Natural Language, so that Language Models could learn it (and cognitive scientists stayed sane)?
David J. Lobina
April 2023
 

The fourth and last entry of a series of posts on Large Language Models (LLMs) and Cognitive Science at 3 Quarks Daily (all to be found here: https://3quarksdaily.com/3quarksdaily/author/davidjlobina), and having previously made the points that Machine/Deep Learning approaches to Artificial Intelligence cannot be smart or sentient, that such approaches are not accounts of cognition anyway, and that when put to the test, LLMs don’t actually behave like human beings at all, the stress in this final say is that a natural language is a sound-meaning mapping, and that this particular fact has clear repercussions for theories of language, of language acquisition, and of language comprehension. Since, therefore, LLMs haven’t learned a natural language or anything remotely similar, LLMs have little to say about linguistic theories stricto sensu, let alone refute any one approach to the study language, the temporary, it is hoped, madness of some cognitive scientists notwithstanding.
