Lexically triggered veridicality inferences
Aaron Steven White
September 2019
 

The use of certain lexical items, such as "know" and "manage", tends to trigger inferences about the factuality of the eventualities or propositions on which those lexical items operate; while the use of others, such as "think" and "want", does not. I review the empirical landscape pertaining to such "lexically triggered veridicality inferences"—focusing in particular on different forms of "factive" and "implicative predicates"—and I discuss various conventionalist and conversationalist approaches to explaining them.
