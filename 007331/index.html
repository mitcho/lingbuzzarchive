The syntax of sluicing-like constructions in English
Jason Overfelt
May 2023
 

This paper investigates understudied sluicing-like constructions in English that arise from embedded wh-in-situ phenomena. I present an analysis of these constructions in which the wh-element becomes a remnant by way of scrambling to the edge of the clause targeted by ellipsis. The analysis serves as evidence for partial covert movement of single in-situ wh-constituents as well as for the proposal that movements that are otherwise covert may be rendered “exceptionally” overt by ellipsis of syntactic structure.
