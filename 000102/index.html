Mobile Inflections in Polish
David Embick
April 2004
 

In this paper I address questions concerning the relationship between morphological/ syntactic form and phonological behavior as they arise in an examination of the 'mobile' or 'floating' inflections of Polish. The mobility of the inflection in question is exhibited in the following minimal pair of sentences, in which -scie, a piece of verbal agreement which indicates a Second-Person Plural subject, may appear either on the verb or on the fronted wh-word.
