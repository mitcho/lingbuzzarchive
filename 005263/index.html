Downstep in Paicî (Oceanic, New Caledonia)
Florian Lionnet
June 2020
 

In this paper, I propose an updated analysis of the tone system of Paicî, one of the rare tonal Oceanic languages. Building on Jean-Claude Rivierre's (Rivierre1974) work, I show that the tonal system of Paicî is best described with three underlying primitives: a High tone, a Low tone, and a downstep /ꜜ/. Paicî is particularly interesting for the empirical documentation as well as the typological and theoretical understanding of downstep, because it combines many rare properties: (i) only downstepped ꜜL is attested; (ii) downstep is its own phonological object; (iii) downstep is realized utterance-initially; and (iv) downstep is not caused by a floating tone, but has its roots in a former accentual system. The paper also provides an acoustic description of tone and downstep in Paicî --based on my own fieldwork as well as some of Rivierre's recordings-- a first step toward filling an important gap in the documentation of downstepped ꜜL tones and their properties.
