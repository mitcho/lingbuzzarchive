Anti-agreeing infinitives in Old Hungarian
Éva Dékány
February 2015
 

This paper explores the distribtuion of Old Hungarian infinitives that bear a 3SG ending but have a non-3SG subject. I call these infinitives 'anti-agreeing infinitives'. I argue that their inflection arises as a result of default agreement, and show that this type of agreement is not attested with infinitives that have a referentially independent subject.
