Parasitic participles in VP-focus pseudoclefts
Jan-Wouter Zwart
July 2023
 

In this article I present an analysis of the properties of parasitic past participles in VP focus pseudoclefts in Dutch (also present in German), in the context of what we know about pseudoclefts more generally and about parasitic participle constructions crosslinguistically. The analysis leads me to consider an ellipsis analysis of the focus VP, which can be shown to have several advantages, especially in the variant of ellipsis espoused by Ott and De Vries (2016), where ellipsis is preceded by fronting of the focused material. However, my conclusion is that the relevant facts can be equally well understood in a ‘base-generation’ analysis, not involving ellipsis. Our discussion shows the importance of the feature ‘anterior’ (as distinct from ‘past’) in the morphosyntactic realization of Continental West Germanic verbs.
