The T-Extension Condition
Ivona Kucerova
May 2010
 

This paper presents a case study of Czech that opens a possibility of unifying various second-position phenomena as instances of an interface condition on head extension. The condition requires a head to undergo at least two instances of merge within its phase. The core of the paper explores properties of T. It is shown that any merge (external or internal, merge of a head or a phrase) yields a well-formed structure. Since it does not matter to the requirement what category merges to T, the condition must be stated as a general requirement on what category may be the root.
