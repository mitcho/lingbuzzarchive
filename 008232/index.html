Presentational nana constructions in Reunion Creole: from broad focus cleft to monoclausal construction
Alina McLellan
February 2024
 

This paper discusses a cluster of related constructions in Reunion Creole involving nana ‘have’. Focusing on a broad focus construction that is functionally equivalent to the il y a-cleft of French, I argue that a once bi-clausal cleft has developed into a monoclausal broad focus construction in Reunion Creole. I present a Role and Reference Grammar analysis of both the bi-clausal cleft and the monoclausal construction, and in the former, I explain how the cleft relative clause differs from restrictive relative clauses. This is the first draft of a paper submitted to Isogloss in February 2024. It is currently under revision.
