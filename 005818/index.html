Scrambling of definite object NPs in Dutch
Hans Broekhuis
October 2023
 

The traditional literature on A-scrambling (object shift) in Dutch normally claims that scrambling of lexical, definite object NPs is sensitive to information structure, e.g. anaphoricity or the focus-presupposition division of the clause (“old” versus “new” information). Over the last 12 years, this position has been disputed on the basis of a series of corpus and experimental investigations, which are claimed to show that lexical definite object NPs resist scrambling. This paper reviews the evidence and argues that the conclusions based on these investigations are partly flawed.
