The paper focuses on the expression of distributivity in Karitiana. It compares distributivity generated by lexical cumulativity, with distributivity generated by distributive numerals. More specifically, this paper studies the syntax and the semantics of reduplicated numerals in Karitiana. We maintain that distributive numerals are not ambiguous between a Determiner Quantifier and an Adverbial Quantifier reading, and that distributivity over the subject and distributivity over occasions are both generated by the same semantics. Our thesis is that distributive numerals are always adverbial pluractional operators - they pluralize the events in the denotation of a relation between events and entities and impose a cardinality restriction on its entities
