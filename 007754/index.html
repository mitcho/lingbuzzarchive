Move and Agree: the view from specificational copular clauses in Kinande
Patricia Schneider-Zioga
December 2023
 

This work provides evidence for an inversion analysis of Specificational Copular Clauses in the Bantu language Kinande, whereby DP1 moves past the initially structurally higher DP2, possibly skipping a low focus projection on its way to the specifier of TP position. A number of interesting agreement patterns emerge in this construction such as the copula agreeing with DP1 in subject position and a low focus head agreeing with the structurally lower focused DP2; the copula can also agree with the focused DP2 under certain circumstances. It is concluded there is both downward Agree and an upward oriented Spec-Head agreement mechanism available in Kinande, which also helps account for the fact that, exceptionally, focused proper names and pronouns cannot value the agreeing low focus head, and failed agreement is expressed instead.
