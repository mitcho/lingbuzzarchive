The interaction of adjectival structure, concord and affixation
Anton Ingason, Einar Sigur√∞sson
October 2016
 

In this paper, we re-examine the case for a split theory of concord (Baier 2015) which distinguishes between concord by Agree and Morphological Feature Copying as well as a split theory of affixation which distinguishes between head-to-head Lowering and Local Dislocation under linear adjacency. We have shown that both complications are redundant once we adopt our structural typology of adjectives. The empirical differences which gave rise to those split theories can in fact be accounted for by systematic variation in the structure of adjectives.
