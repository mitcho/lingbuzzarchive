THE ORIGIN OF LANGUAGE: decoding the tagalog grid and its rule of four
Luis Umali Stuart
May 2022
 

the origin of language in humans has ever been a mystery to all thinkers on the subject over the centuries. it is a major unsolved problem in linguistics. in these latest readings of the tagalog-english grid, we are led to a theory of it, bringing together the evidence of mathematics, biology, and biochemistry in an evolution story of language unexpectedly quantum in its conclusions. the tale of the grid’s discovery and the logic that drives it is told in essentially three reports archived at lingbuzz—‘the grid of language’ (000996), the ‘amazing rule of four’ (002823), and ‘the perfection of um’ (005807). here presented is an overview of a fourth report (now an ebook of the same title at lulu.com). excerpted here are the very latest versions of the UM, UM, IN, and IN grids; my first crack at the eight 2x2 permutations behind the eight quadrisections of the grid; and an exercise in gridding to challenge the inclined. kept for the ebook is the section ‘the language of nature’ that tracks the natural origins of language by a backward trail of quadrisections leading from the nervous system to the first foursome of all—in a fitting climax to the grid quartet.
