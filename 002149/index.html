Parametric and nonparametric approach in the analysis of stress typologies
Marc Van Oostendorp
July 2014
 

The typology of stress systems presents probably one of the most succesfull applications of the application of formal grammatical theory to typological issues: we dispose of a wide array of data from many different languages, and both parametric and non-parametric (constraint-ranking) approaches have been succesfully applied to them. This paper gives an overview of this discussion, with a focus on the theoretical issues involved. It is aimed therefore not only to phonologists working on stress, but also to other linguists interested in how formal linguistics interfaces with linguistic typology.
