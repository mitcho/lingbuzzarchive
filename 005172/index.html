Same and Different: A presuppositional account
Daniel Hardt, Line Hove Mikkelsen
April 2020
 

Same and different give rise to so-called internal readings (Every boy read the same/a different book.). These are analysed in terms of distributive quantification by Brasoveanu (2011), who invokes special mechanisms including stacks and an index offset. We propose that internal readings arise from accommodation of an eventuality presupposition. This account covers the same empirical ground as Brasoveanu's, while dispensing with special mechanisms in favor of general constraints on presupposition binding and accommodation.
