Person restrictions in South Baffin Inuktitut: An argument for feature movement
Michelle Yuan
September 2015
 

The South Baffin dialect of Inuktitut exhibits an apparent person restriction that bans 1st/2nd person (though not 3rd person) agreement morphology from co-occurring with oblique case. I argue that, contrary to surface appearances, this phenomenon is not actually a restriction on 1st/2nd person features; rather, it is a byproduct of moving the phi-features out of the agreement head and into the head hosting oblique case. The broader theoretical claim is that feature movement is a possible means of valuation by Agree.
