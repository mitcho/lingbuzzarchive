Implicatures from Silence: A remark on Chung's puzzle
Matthew Barros, Andr√©s Saab
March 2016
 

We focus on the observation that the interpretation of some elliptical utterances does not match that made available by the putative non-elliptical alternant. We propose an account of this couched in the assumption that ellipsis involves non- insertion of (otherwise) pronounced material in the PF-branch (Merchant 2005, Saab 2008, amongst others). The capacity for ellipsis to give rise to implicatures, unavailable in the absence of ellipsis, follows from standard and independently motivated assumptions about the featural content of pronounced lexical items (as opposed to elided/unpronounced items, extending ideas in Kratzer 1998, 2009, Safir 2014). Our approach is consistent with a view of the identity condition on ellipsis resolution that crucially incorporates a semantic/interpretive component.
