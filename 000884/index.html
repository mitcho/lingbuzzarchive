Lexicalizing number and gender in Colonnata
Tarald Taraldsen
June 2009
 

This squib uses some facts about the Lunigiana varieties of Italian as described by Manzini & Savoia (2005) to build an argument in favor of the view that a single vocabulary item may lexicalize a non-trivial subtree. The analysis also provides arguments in favor of the Superset Principle as well as for two ancillary principles governing vocabulary insertion, one of which is a version of the familiar "Elsewhere Principle".
