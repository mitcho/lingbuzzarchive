Two kinds of bans on ergative extraction
Norvin Richards
March 2023
 

I argue that there are two kinds of bans on ergative extraction. One is characteristic of predicate-initial ergative languages, and typically involves a ban on both wh-movement and relativization, specifically of ergatives; the other is characteristic of predicate-final ergative languages, and specifically bans relativization, of any element which is not absolutive. For the first ban, we see that for languages with overt morphology signalling the transitivity of the verb, we can predict whether ergative extraction is banned from morphological and phonological properties of the transitivity morphology. I develop an account of the ban, and the relation to transitivity morphology, using existing proposals of Contiguity Theory. For the second kind of ban, I propose an account involving a condition on case concord; again, we can determine from the morphology of the verbs of relative clauses in a given language whether or not it invokes this kind of ban.
