The Role of Contrast in Locality: Transparent Palatal Glides in Kyrghyz
Andrew Ira Nevins, Bert Vaux
August 2005
 

This paper discusses the role of contrast in determining the locality of [back] harmony. Specifically, we argue that palatal glides, although they are phonetically [-back], are transparent to [+back] harmony across them, because harmony is relativized only to contrastive values of the feature [back]. We argue that the feature distinguishing high vowels and glides is [Â± consonantal], and that a relativized theory of locality provides a falsifiable and restrictive theory of variation in the class of harmony participants throughout Turkic.
