Noun Incorporation as Symmetry Breaking
Michael Barrie
July 2010
 

This paper proposes a novel account of noun incorporation in Northern Iroquoian. It is proposed that there is no special mechanism for noun incorporation and that this phenomenon falls out naturally from the geometry of the phrase structure under MoroÂ’s theory of Dynamic Antisymmetry. In a nutshell, when the verbal head and the nominal head undergo Merge, they form a point of symmetric c-command, which is resolved by the nominal head moving to the specifier of the verb phrase. Further, it is proposed that in noun incorporation constructions with a full DP double the incorporated noun and the DP form a constituent, which is merged in theta-position.
