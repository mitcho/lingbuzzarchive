On the duality of patterning
Jordi Fortuny
May 2010
 

A well-known and uncontroversially universal property of natural languages is what Charles F. Hockett called duality or duality of patterning. This property can be expressed in the following terms: linguistic expressions can be decomposed into minimal meaningful discrete units commonly called morphemes that in turn can be decomposed into minimal distinctive though meaningless discrete units called phonemes. Here we study how certain conditions mainly related to mathematical information theory and structural complexity favor the emergence of the duality of patterning.
