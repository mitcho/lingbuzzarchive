Against 'Tanglewood' by focus movement: a reply to Erlewine and Kotek (to appear)
Nicholas Longenbaugh, Itai Bassi
June 2017
 

We reply to Erlewine and Kotek's (to appear) recent claim that the phenomenon of co-variation under focus (`Tanglewood' sentences, Kratzer 1991) is subject to syntactic islands (contra Kratzer), and that it should therefore be handled by a focus-movement theory. We show that Erlewine and Kotek's main arguments are confounded, and that once the confounds are removed, the arguments either fail to go through or go against their account.
