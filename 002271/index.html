Sentential Subjects and the Minimal Labeling Algorithm
Stamatogiannis Nickolas
September 2014
 

This paper adopts Chomsky's (2013, 2014a, 2014b) latest formulation of the Minimal Labeling Algorithm, so as to derive the specific aspects of the distribution of sentential subjects. I will argue that besides sentential subjects that qualify as topicalized elements (cf. Koster 1978), there exist contexts where they are part of the main syntactic derivation (cf. Davies & Dubinsky 2009). Moreover, I will try to show how the categorical nature of sentential subjects interacts with their position in the clausal spine, paying particular attention on how labels are assigned in narrow syntax. As will be shown, such an approach is superior to previous ones in that it covers not only the standard cases but also some new and unnoticed facts.
