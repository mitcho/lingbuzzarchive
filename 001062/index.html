High Applicatives are not High Enough: A Cartographic Solution
Wei-Tien Dylan Tsai
April 2010
 

This paper examines a cluster of phenomena in Mandarin Chinese, which may collectively be characterized as "affective constructions". Based on the diversified and sometimes hybrid nature of these affective construals, we argue that the usual dichotomy between high and low applicatives is not enough to capture their syntactic and semantic idiosyncrasies. We will need a more elaborate theory to map out the topography of applicatives under the Cartographic Approach (Rizzi 1997, Cinque 1999). It is shown that Chinese affectives have an unusual distribution extending far beyond the boundary of the vP phase, which in turn argues for an independent applicative projection in the left periphery, presumably associated with speaker-orientedness and a presupposition of unexpectedness.
