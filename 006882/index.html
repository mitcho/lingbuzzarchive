Chinese Activity Constructions: Light Verb Syntax and Adnominalization
Keith Tse
August 2022
 

Chinese Activity Constructions, which commonly use the noun phrase headed by adnominaliser de (的) and its dialectal g-forms (e.g. Cantonese ge3 嘅), display dialectal microvariation which can be derived from their etymological origins. It has been well noted that while the Activity phrase headed by de (e.g. 他的老師 'he being a teacher') can occur in both subject and object position (他當他的老師 'he is being a teacher'/他的老師當得好 'he is being a good teacher'), dialectal g-forms can only occur in object but not in subject position (e.g. Cantonese 佢做佢嘅老師 'he is being a teacher'/*佢嘅老師做得好 'he is being a good teacher'). This paper examines the microvariation in Chinese nominalisation and the microparametric variation which underlies the historical-comparative distribution of Chinese Activity Constructions.
