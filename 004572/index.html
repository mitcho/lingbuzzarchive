Nothing's wrong with believing (or hoping) whether
Aaron Steven White
April 2019
 

Theories of clause selection that aim to explain the distribution of interrogative and declarative complement clauses often take as a starting point that predicates like "think", "believe", "hope", and "fear" are incompatible with interrogative complements. I give corpus evidence that this is not the case, thereby substantially weakening the generalizations these theories are founded on.
