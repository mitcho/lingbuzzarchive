Monotone Amazement
Rick Nouwen
November 2005
 

This paper investigates how evaluative predicates (like amazing, surprising etc.) combine with monotone and non-monotone statements. I argue that although these predicates give rise to different interpretations in different positions, they always licence the same polar orientation effects. Additionally, I argue that these effects are solely due to the monotonicity of evaluative predicates which preserves (or reverses) the entailments that are licensed by the structure to which the predicate applies. Finally, the different ways in which evaluative predicates convey their emotive content differ with respect to factivity. As I will show, adverbs like amazingly can modify operators that convey a standard of comparison. When this is the case, factivity is lost.
