Successive cyclicity, word order and the double-V2 rule in Dinka and Mòcheno
Federica Cognola
June 2015
 

The parallels in the distribution of OV/VO word orders, extraction and cyclic movement between the two fully unrelated languages Dinka (Nilo-Sarahan, South Sudan) and Mòcheno (Germanic, Northern Italy) are shown to follow from the presence of an EPP feature on both C° and V° which forces one XP to form a Spec/head configuration with the verb in both phases (double V2 rule). The asymmetries between them derive from the fact that the same syntactic structure is associated with different semantics in the two languages, and that low V2 always involves the non-finite verb form in Mòcheno, and both finite and non-finite verb forms in Dinka.
