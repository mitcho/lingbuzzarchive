Levels of Linguistic Acts and the Semantics of Saying and Quoting
Friederike Moltmann
September 2016
 

This papers argues that Austin's notion of a locutionary act is extremely well-reflected in the semantics of natural language. It develops a new semantics of verbs saying (say, think, write) as well as of direct quotation within the overall view that sentences act semantically as predicates of attitudinal and modal objects. The paper outlines a novel compositional semantics of quotation based on three-dimensional syntactic structures, permitting the integration of lower-level linguistic structures (say, phonological or morphological structures) into the syntactic input to an act-based semantic interpretation.
