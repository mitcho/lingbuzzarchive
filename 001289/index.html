Syntactic doubling and deletion as a source of variation
Sjef Barbiers
October 2010
 

The paper argues that syntactic doubling is a core property of syntax required to express monadic and dyadic predication. Much of syntactic doubling does not surface, because syntactic doubling introduces local (feature) redundancy that triggers a process of partial or full deletion. This set of assumptions is demonstrated to explain the complex patterns of variation found in Dutch focus particle doubling.
