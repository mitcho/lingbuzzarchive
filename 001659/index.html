Fives Notes on Correlatives
Guglielmo Cinque
November 2009
 

The article attempts to show that correlatives (at least those that do not contain multiple wh-phrases) are embedded in a DP which is left dislocated at the beginning of the matrix clause and is resumed by a correlative pro-form (or a full DP) inside the matrix clause. The left dislocated DP may contain, depending on the language, either an externally headed postnominal, or an externally headed prenominal, or an internally headed, or a headless (free), relative clause (whence the conclusion that correlatives are not an independent, fifth, type of relative construction. The article also documents the existence of non-restrictive correlatives, and argues that multiple correlatives are not relative clauses at all.
