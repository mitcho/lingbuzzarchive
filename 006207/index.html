Types of Disjunction: Negative Scope
Ayaka Tamura, Yoichi Miyamoto, Uli Sauerland
September 2021
 

We discuss how disjunction interacts semantically and syntactically with downward entailing environments. In many languages, disjunction exhibits a positive polarity behavior. Different accounts of this have been offered by Goro (2007), Shibata (2015) and Nicolae (2017). We introduce a difference in predictions the three account make. The relevant data concern rescuing of positive polarity items in a relative clauses restricting a universal quantifier. We then report an experimental study on this difference. We argue that the result is consistent only with the account of Nicolae (2017). The result that shows that Japanese disjunction is more alike disjunction in other languages (specifically, French), and also illustrates how complex scopal difference can be investigated experimentally.
