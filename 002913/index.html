N1-of-N1 Constructions? Is There Such Thing?
Adina Camelia Bleotu
January 2011
 

This paper deals with the nominal phrase ‘băiat de băiat’ (‘boy of boy’) and similar phrases in Romanian, an N-of-N construction, in which the first noun is the same as the second noun. Such constructions are not to be analysed as qualitative pseudopartitives, given the fact that, unlike in the case of pseudopartitives, it is the first noun that acts as the syntactic/ semantic head, and not the second. Instead, the paper proposes a different analysis of these constructions, starting from Kayne (1994, 2005)’s antisymmetry of syntax and his theory of silent nouns and from Zamparelli (2000)’s division of the nominal domain into three layers SDP (Strong Determiner Phrase), PDP (Predicate Determiner Phrase), KIP (Kind Phrase). The proposed analysis accounts for the type interpretation of the second noun: [RP [KP băiat] de [KIP [TIP] [KI’ băiat]]] (‘boy of TYPE boy’).
